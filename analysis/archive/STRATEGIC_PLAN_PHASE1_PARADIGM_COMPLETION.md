## Executive Summary

Phase 1 systematically analyzed three morphological paradigms (S-R, D-R, K-P) to determine if they exhibit the same vowel alternation semantic system as the K-R paradigm (KU-RO/KI-RO).

**Key Findings**:

| Paradigm | Members | Total Occurrences | K-R Similarity | Status | Promotion |
|----------|---------|-------------------|----------------|--------|-----------|
| **S-R** | 16 | 51 | 80% | HIGH | SA-RA2 remains PROBABLE |
| **D-R** | 17 | 29 | 80% | HIGH | DA-RE promoted to POSSIBLE |
| **K-P** | 12+ | 60+ | 40% | MEDIUM | KU-PA3-NU at PROBABLE |

**Critical Discovery**: The K-P paradigm does NOT show U/I semantic polarity like K-R. Instead, KU-PA forms show different first-syllable vowels without clear functional differentiation.

**Recommendation**:

- KA-PA: Add as **POSSIBLE** (header position pattern)
- K-P paradigm: Does NOT show K-R-style semantic system

## 4. Cross-Paradigm Synthesis

### 4.1 Vowel Alternation Comparison

| Paradigm | U-forms | I-forms | A-forms | U/I Polarity? |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|---------------|
| **K-R** | KU-RO (37) | KI-RO (16) | KA-RU (2) | **YES** (total/deficit) |
| **S-R** | SA-RU (6) | SI-RU (2) | SA-RA2 (40) | **NO** |
| **D-R** | DU-RA (1) | DI-RI-NA (2) | DA-RE (7) | **NO** |
| **K-P** | KU-PA (42+) | KI-PA (2) | KA-PA (11) | **NO** |

### 4.2 Key Conclusion

**The U/I total/deficit semantic polarity is K-R SPECIFIC**, not a universal Linear A feature.

Other paradigms show:
- First syllable vowel variation WITHOUT clear semantic function
- A-series dominance in S-R (SA-)
- U-series dominance in K-P (KU-)
- Mixed distribution in D-R

### 4.3 What the K-P Data Shows

The K-P paradigm is NOT parallel to K-R because:

1. **No KI-PA deficit marker**: Only 2 JA-KI-PA3 compounds exist
2. **KU- dominance**: 70% of forms are KU-, not 50/50 like K-R
3. **KU-PA3-NU is likely a name/title**: Wide numeric range (1-109) suggests personnel
4. **KA-PA header pattern**: Similar to KA-RU but rare
