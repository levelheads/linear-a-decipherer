## Executive Summary

Phase 6 systematically investigated the 30 paradigm candidates identified in Phase 1. Beyond the well-established K-R paradigm (KU-RO/KI-RO), we identified several promising morphological patterns, with the **S-R paradigm** (SA-RA2, SA-RU, SA-RO, SA-MA) emerging as the most significant secondary system.

**Key Findings**:
1. **S-R Paradigm**: 42 attestations across 8 variants - strong functional pattern
2. **D-R Paradigm**: 33 attestations - potential personal name/title system
3. **Vowel-final Endings**: -RO, -RA2, -RU, -RE function as grammatical markers
4. **No True Triplets Found**: Inflectional system may be simpler than expected

## II. S-R Paradigm Analysis (SA-RA2 Family)

### 2.1 Form Inventory

| Form | Count | Contexts |
|------|-------|----------|
| **SA-RA2** | 20 | Always before logograms (GRA, OLE, CYP) |
| **SA-RU** | 6 | Before quantities, near totaling sections |
| **SA-MA** | 4 | Before numerals |
| **SA-RO** | 4 | Before logograms (OLE, VIN) |
| **SA-RA** | 2 | Before TE separator |
| **SA-VIN** | 2 | Ligature with VIN logogram |
| **SA-SA-RA-ME** | 2 | Religious context (peak sanctuaries) |
| **SA-MI-DA-E** | 2 | Administrative context |

### 2.2 Contextual Analysis

**SA-RA2 (20 occurrences)**:
```
HT18: *304 3 SA-RA2 GRA 10
HT28a: OLE+TU [frac] SA-RA2 OLE+DI 1
HT28b: U-MI-NA-SI [sep] SA-RA2 GRA 20
HT30: SI 4 SA-RA2 CYP 14
HT114a: KI-RI-TA2 SA-RA2 GRA
```

**Pattern**: SA-RA2 consistently appears:
- **Before commodity logograms** (GRA, OLE, CYP, VIN)
- **After syllabic words or separators**
- **Never at list-final position** (unlike KU-RO)

**SA-RU (6 occurrences)**:
```
HT86a: GRA+K+L 20 SA-RU 20 DI-DE-RU
HT94b: KE-KI-RU 1 SA-RU 1 KU-RO
HT95a: MI-NU-TE 10 SA-RU 20 KU-NI-SU
```

**Pattern**: SA-RU appears:
- **Before numerals**
- **Near KU-RO totaling sections**
- **In longer commodity lists**

### 2.3 Functional Hypothesis

| Hypothesis | Evidence For | Evidence Against |
|------------|-------------|------------------|
| SA-RA2 = "allocation" (*sharaku*) | Position before logograms; semantic fit | Appears with varied commodities |
| SA- = Article/Determiner | Consistent initial position | Would expect more frequent use |
| SA-R* = Transaction type | -RA2, -RU, -RO endings parallel K-R pattern | No clear alternation rule |

### 2.4 Four-Hypothesis Test

#### Luwian/Anatolian
- No clear Luwian cognate for SA-RA2
- -RA2 ending not characteristic of Luwian morphology
- **Verdict**: WEAK support

#### Semitic (Akkadian)
- SA-RA2 could represent Akkadian **sharaku** "to give, allocate"
- The root SH-R-K (*sharaku* D-stem = "to grant, bestow")
- Semantic fit: allocation before commodity listing
- **Verdict**: MODERATE support

#### Pre-Greek Substrate
- SA- prefix resembles Pre-Greek intensive/collective marker
- -RA2 ending common in Pre-Greek toponyms
- **Verdict**: POSSIBLE (no clear semantics)

#### Proto-Greek
- No obvious Greek cognate
- SA-RA does not match Greek administrative vocabulary
- **Verdict**: WEAK support

### 2.5 S-R Paradigm Conclusion

**Confidence**: PROBABLE (Level 4-5)

The SA- paradigm shows genuine morphological variation (-RA2, -RU, -RO, -MA). Unlike the K-R paradigm (totaling function), SA- appears to mark **transaction type or allocation status**. The Akkadian *sharaku* hypothesis remains the most semantically coherent.

**Critical Observation**: SA-RA2 (20x) and KU-RO (37x) are the two most frequent non-numeric, non-logographic words in the administrative corpus, suggesting they represent **complementary administrative functions**.

### 3.4 D-R Paradigm Conclusion

**Confidence**: POSSIBLE (Level 5)

The DA- forms likely represent **personal names or titles** rather than a unified grammatical paradigm. The sealing-exclusive DA-KA pattern suggests authentication function.

## VIII. Linguistic Hypothesis Synthesis

### 8.1 Morphological Typology

Based on paradigm analysis, Linear A shows:
- **Suffixing preference**: Endings more prominent than prefixes
- **R-vowel suffix system**: -RO, -RA2, -RU, -RA as core markers
- **Limited productive inflection**: 2-3 forms per root typical
- **Agglutinative tendencies**: Suffix stacking (KU-PA3-NA-TU)

### 8.2 Hypothesis Compatibility

| Agglutination | Compatible | Partial | Unknown | Incompatible |
