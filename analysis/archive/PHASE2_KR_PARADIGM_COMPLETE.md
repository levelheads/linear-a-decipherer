## Key Questions Answered

### 1. Is there a vowel alternation pattern (U vs I vs A)?

**YES**. Clear pattern emerging:
- U-series (KU-RO, KU-RE, KU-RA): Positive accounting (totals, subtotals)
- I-series (KI-RO, KI-RA, KI-RU): Negative accounting (deficits, owed)
- A-series (KA-RU): Header/category function (tablet-initial)

### 2. Do different vowels indicate different functions?

**YES**. Evidence supports functional distinctions:
- KU-RO (37) = Total (list-final)
- KI-RO (16) = Deficit (variable position)
- KU-RE (2) = Subtotal? (before KU-RO)
- KA-RU (2) = Header (list-initial)

### 3. Is KU-RE a subtotal vs KU-RO grand total?

**PROBABLY YES**. HT39 shows:
```
... 10 | KU-RE | KU-RO *414+A 100
```
KU-RE appears IMMEDIATELY before KU-RO, suggesting hierarchical relationship.

### 4. Are KI-RA and KU-RA variants or distinct terms?

**LIKELY VARIANTS**. Both:
- Have -A final vowel (distinct from -O forms)
- Appear infrequently (2 each)
- May represent case/gender variants of primary forms
- KU-RA appears at non-HT sites (possible regional variant)

## Research Implications

1. **Vowel alternation encodes function**: This is evidence for a morphological system in Minoan, with vowels marking semantic/grammatical distinctions.

2. **Sophisticated accounting**: The K-R paradigm suggests Minoan had a developed accounting terminology with terms for totals, subtotals, deficits, and categories.

3. **Semitic connection strengthened**: All K-R forms are compatible with Semitic *kull/*kol and *gara roots, with native Minoan inflection patterns.

4. **Regional variation**: KU-RA at ZA/ARKH vs. KU-RO at HT may indicate dialectal or scribal variation.

5. **Three-tier hierarchy**: PO-TO-KU-RO > KU-RO > KU-RE suggests a hierarchical totaling system.
