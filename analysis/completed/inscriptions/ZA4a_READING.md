## Novel Observations

### 1. E-*82 as Institutional Mega-Recipient

E-*82 receives 50 units -- nearly half the total. This is the second-largest single-recipient allocation in the Zakros corpus (after *34-JU-TE-MI's 180 on ZA6a). The combination of undeciphered sign *82 with E- prefix may designate an institution rather than a person.

### 2. Zakros Recipient Network

The 50% overlap between ZA4a and ZA15a establishes a documented recipient network at Zakros. These individuals (QE-SI-ZU-E, *28B-NU-MA-RE, SI-PI-KI, KA-DI) received distributions on multiple occasions, indicating they were regular participants in the Zakros administrative system -- likely local officials, estate holders, or institutional representatives.

### 3. KA-DI Fixed Allocation Confirmed

KA-DI = 3 on both ZA4a and ZA15b. This fixed quantity at the same site across different tablets strongly suggests a standardized allocation: either a fixed tribute from/to KA-DI or a standard ration. The consistency is too precise to be coincidental.

### 4. Different Allocation Ratios Between Tablets

QE-SI-ZU-E receives 4 on ZA4a but 57 on ZA15a. *28B-NU-MA-RE receives 13 on ZA4a but 6 on ZA15a. This dramatic variation in allocation sizes between tablets sharing the same recipients suggests different commodities, different transaction types, or different time periods.
