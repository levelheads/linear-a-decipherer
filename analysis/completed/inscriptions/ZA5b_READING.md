## Novel Observations

### 1. Domain Layering at the Personal Name Level

ZA5b provides the clearest example of domain layering at the onomastic (personal name) level. SI-PI-KI (Semitic SUPPORTED 4.15) and MA-KA-I-TA (Luwian POSSIBLE 1.5) appear as recipients on the same tablet. Previous domain-layering evidence has primarily involved:
- Admin vocabulary (Semitic: A-DU, KU-RO, SA-RA₂) vs. morphological suffixes (Luwian: -JA, -TE, -WA)
- Religious vocabulary (Luwian/Pre-Greek) vs. economic terms (Semitic)

ZA5b adds a third domain-layering dimension: Semitic-type and Luwian-type personal names co-existing in the same administrative context. This is consistent with a multilingual or multi-ethnic society where individuals with names from different linguistic traditions participated in shared economic institutions.

### 2. SI-PI-KI: Strongest Semitic Signal at Zakros

SI-PI-KI's Semitic score of 4.15 is exceptional for Zakros. Most Zakros names lean Luwian (*28B-NU-MA-RE, QE-SI-ZU-E) or are indeterminate. SI-PI-KI stands out as a potential Semitic loan-name, possibly indicating:
- Trade contact between Zakros and Semitic-speaking regions (Levant, Egypt)
- A Semitic-origin individual in the Zakros economic network
- A coincidental phonological match (always a possibility with short names)

The 3-tablet attestation (ZA4a, ZA5b, ZA15a) confirms this is a real name in the administrative system, not a misreading.

### 3. *28B-NU-MA-RE: Major Zakros Figure

*28B-NU-MA-RE receives allocations on 3 Zakros tablets (13, 20, 6 units respectively -- total 39 units across documents). On ZA5b, it receives 82% of the total allocation. This scale of documentation suggests a major institutional entity or high-ranking individual at Zakros, comparable to:
- DA-SI-*118 at Hagia Triada (3 tablets, 45 total units)
- TE-TU at Hagia Triada (2 tablets, 57 total units)

The *28B logographic element remains a puzzle -- it may be a title, a commodity qualifier, or an institutional designator that distinguishes this compound name from plain NU-MA-RE.

### 4. High Readiness, Low Complexity

ZA5b has the highest readiness score (0.501) among the three Zakros readings in this campaign, despite having only 3 entries. The high score reflects:
- All three names are cross-tablet attested (high verification potential)
- Two names have strong hypothesis results (SI-PI-KI 4.15, MA-KA-I-TA 1.5)
