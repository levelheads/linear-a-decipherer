## Structural Analysis

### Document Type

**Abbreviated libation formula on stone vessel at Iouktas peak sanctuary**

IOZa9 preserves two elements of the standard 6-position libation formula: the divine name/sacred object (JA-SA-SA-RA-ME) and the subordinate verb (U-NA-KA-NA-SI). The main verb (A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA), place name, dedicant, and prepositional phrase are absent -- either abbreviated or on a damaged portion.

### Document Structure

```
[Pos 4]  JA-SA-SA-RA-ME           Divine name / sacred object
         𐄁                        Word divider
[Pos 5]  U-NA-KA-NA-SI            Verb: "to dedicate" / ritual action
```

### Comparison with Full Libation Formula

| Position | Function | IOZa9 | Standard (IO Za 2) | PKZa11 (Form B) |
|----------|----------|-------|---------------------|------------------|
| 1 | Main verb | **ABSENT** | A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA | A-TA-I-*301-WA-E |
| 2 | Place name | **ABSENT** | (varies) | DI-KI-TE |
| 3 | Dedicant | **ABSENT** | (varies) | (varies) |
| 4 | Divine/object | **JA-SA-SA-RA-ME** | JA-SA-SA-RA-ME | SA-SA-RA-ME |
| 5 | Verb | **U-NA-KA-NA-SI** | U-NA-KA-NA-SI | U-NA-RU-KA-NA-TI |
| 6 | Prepositional | **ABSENT** | SI-RU-TE + I-PI-NA-MA | SI-RU |

### Notable Structural Features

1. **Abbreviated formula**: Only positions 4 and 5 preserved. This could reflect:
   - Abbreviation (the scribe considered positions 4-5 sufficient)
   - Damage (positions 1-3 and 6 lost)
   - A variant formula tradition at Iouktas
2. **Standard Form A**: Both terms match Form A (JA-SA-SA-RA-ME with JA- prefix; U-NA-KA-NA-SI with -SI suffix), not Form B (SA-SA-RA-ME; U-NA-RU-KA-NA-TI). This places IOZa9 in the "principal" formula tradition.
3. **Word divider**: 𐄁 between the two terms confirms they are separate lexical units.
4. **No logograms or numerals**: Purely religious/dedicatory text with no administrative content.
5. **Peak sanctuary context**: Iouktas is one of the major Minoan peak sanctuaries, consistent with libation formula use.

## What We Know For Certain

1. **Formula membership**: Both terms are standard elements of the Minoan libation formula. JA-SA-SA-RA-ME is position 4; U-NA-KA-NA-SI is position 5.
2. **Cross-site attestation**: Both terms attested at multiple peak sanctuary and religious sites (IO, PK, SY, TL, KO).
3. **Form A**: This is the "principal" form of the libation formula (JA- prefix on SA-SA-RA-ME; -SI suffix on U-NA-KA-NA-SI), not Form B.
4. **Religious context**: Iouktas is a peak sanctuary. Stone vessel support confirms ritual/dedicatory function.
5. **Abbreviated**: Only 2 of 6 formula positions represented.

## What We Hypothesize

1. **JA-SA-SA-RA-ME as divine name**: PROBABLE. The geminated -SA-SA- and -ME suffix suggest Pre-Greek substrate phonology. Whether this names a specific deity or a sacred object (offering vessel, altar) is unresolved.
2. **U-NA-KA-NA-SI as verbal**: PROBABLE. The KA-NA root paradigm and -SI suffix strongly suggest a verbal form with Luwian morphological influence. Possible meaning: "to dedicate," "to offer," "to consecrate."
3. **Abbreviated vs. damaged**: We cannot determine whether the missing formula positions (1-3, 6) were never inscribed or were lost to damage. The stone vessel support makes damage possible.
4. **Two-register pattern**: Religious vocabulary at this site confirms the two-register hypothesis: administrative vocabulary favors Semitic, religious vocabulary favors Luwian/Pre-Greek.

## Novel Observations

### 1. Iouktas Multi-Formula Attestation

IOZa9 is the third Iouktas libation inscription in the project's record (after IO Za 2 and IO Za 6). The peak sanctuary has the densest concentration of libation formulas in the corpus, consistent with its role as a major cult center. The abbreviated form (positions 4-5 only) may indicate a more concise dedicatory tradition alongside the full 6-position formula.

### 2. Formula Abbreviation Pattern

The reduction to positions 4-5 (divine name + verb) suggests these are the "core" of the formula -- the essential elements for a valid dedication. Positions 1-3 (invocation + place + dedicant) and position 6 (prepositional) may be contextually implied at a peak sanctuary where the location and ritual context are self-evident.

### 3. Form A Consistency at Iouktas

All Iouktas libation inscriptions use Form A (JA- prefix on SA-SA-RA-ME; -SI suffix on verb), not Form B. Form B is attested at Palaikastro (PK Za 11). This may indicate:
- Regional formula traditions (Iouktas = Form A; Palaikastro = Form B)
- Temporal variation (Form A and B from different periods)
- Functional variation (Form A and B for different ritual contexts)
