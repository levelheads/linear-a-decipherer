## The KA Question: Commodity Logogram Analysis

The most distinctive feature of HT 11b is the repeated appearance of KA. It occurs 5 times:
- As compound *86-KA (Line 3)
- Standalone twice (Lines 4, 5)
- After recipient names (Lines 6, 7)

### Evidence That KA Functions as a Commodity Logogram on This Tablet

1. **Position**: KA precedes quantities in all 5 entries, exactly as GRA, VIN, OLE do on commodity tablets.

2. **Lines 6-7 structure**: `RU-ZU-NA KA 30` and `SA-QE-RI 𐄁 KA 30` follow the pattern NAME + COMMODITY + QUANTITY, identical to entries like `DA-SI-*118 VIN 19` on HT 13.

3. **Lines 4-5**: Standalone KA + quantity (30, 50) parallels how commodities appear when the recipient name is omitted or implicit.

4. **Arithmetic**: All KA-marked quantities sum to KU-RO, confirming they measure the same thing.

5. **Corpus parallels**: KA appears on HT 140 alongside OLE variants (`KA 4`, `KA 1`, `KA 1` interspersed with `OLE+U` entries), suggesting KA is a commodity distinct from OLE. On HT 93a, KA appears at the end (`KA 4`) alongside GRA+PA, VIR+[?], suggesting a separate commodity category. On HT 85b, KA appears with quantity 1 in a personnel list.

6. **Scale**: The quantities (30-50 per entry, 180 total) are large but not unprecedented. HT 13 has VIN 130.5; HT 85a has VIR 66. The scale suggests a bulk commodity rather than a luxury item.

### What Commodity Might KA Represent?

| Candidate | Evidence | Against |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Grain (generic)** | Large quantities consistent with grain; KA-RO-PA₃ (vessel, Akkadian *karpu*) has KA- prefix | GRA logogram exists separately |
| **Barley** | SA-QE-RI ~ Akkadian *sheu* "barley" (very tentative) | Extremely speculative |
| **Wool/textile units** | MA-RU = wool exists; KA could be a textile grade | No pictographic support |
| **Unknown commodity** | KA may represent a commodity not identifiable from Linear B parallels | Most conservative reading |

**Assessment**: KA functions as a commodity logogram on HT 11b (PROBABLE), but its specific commodity identity is UNKNOWN. It appears distinct from the established logograms (GRA, VIN, OLE, VIR, CYP). The large quantities (30-50 per entry) suggest a bulk, divisible commodity measured in whole units.

## Structural Analysis

### Document Type

**Large-scale single-commodity (KA) distribution tablet**

HT 11b records the distribution of 180 units of an unknown commodity (KA) across 5 entries, from (or categorized under) DE-NU with sub-category RU-RA₂. The large quantities (30-50 per entry) and integer-only values indicate a bulk commodity.

### Document Structure

```
[H]   DE-NU                         Header: institutional source/category
[?]   RU-RA₂                        Sub-header: qualifier (no quantity)
[R]   *86-KA              40        Entry 1: *86 compound, 40 units KA
[R?]  KA                  30        Entry 2: unnamed recipient, 30 units KA
[R?]  KA                  50        Entry 3: unnamed recipient, 50 units KA (largest)
[R]   RU-ZU-NA   KA       30        Entry 4: RU-ZU-NA, 30 units KA
[R]   SA-QE-RI 𐄁 KA       30        Entry 5: SA-QE-RI, 30 units KA
[T]   KU-RO              180        Total: 180 units KA
```

### Notable Structural Features

1. **Arithmetic perfection**: 40 + 30 + 50 + 30 + 30 = 180 exactly. Class A verification.

2. **Two entry types**: Lines 3-5 have no explicit recipient name before KA (or *86-KA as compound). Lines 6-7 have explicit recipient names (RU-ZU-NA, SA-QE-RI) before KA. This may indicate:
   - Lines 3-5: Sub-categories of the RU-RA₂ header (institutional allocations, not personal)
   - Lines 6-7: Named recipients receiving personal allocations

3. **Separator on Line 7**: The word separator (𐄁) between SA-QE-RI and KA is unique on this tablet. On Line 6, RU-ZU-NA KA 30 has no separator. This may be a scribal convention distinguishing the final entry, or separating the name from the commodity indicator.

4. **Scale**: 180 total units is among the largest KU-RO values in the HT corpus. Compare: HT 85a (66 VIR), HT 13 (130.5 VIN), HT 122 (97 VIR). Only HT 13 approaches this scale, and that involves fractions.

5. **Mostly hapax vocabulary**: DE-NU, RU-RA₂, *86-KA, RU-ZU-NA, and SA-QE-RI all appear only on HT 11b. This tablet is isolated in the vocabulary network.

6. **Same scribe as HT 11a**: Both sides written by HT Scribe 24 from Villa Magazine. Side a (HT 11a) has a different structure: A-RU-RA header, 3 recipients with KU-RO 10, then post-total entries including VIR+[?]-I and TA₂ 15.

### Alternative Structural Reading

It is possible that *86-KA is a compound recipient name (not *86 modifying KA-commodity), and the standalone KA entries (Lines 4-5) are abbreviated recipient names. Under this reading:

```
DE-NU                  Header: category
RU-RA₂                 Sub-header
*86-KA          40     Recipient *86-KA: 40
KA              30     Recipient KA: 30
KA              50     Recipient KA: 50 (same person, different allocation?)
RU-ZU-NA  KA    30     Recipient RU-ZU-NA KA: 30 (compound name?)
SA-QE-RI  KA    30     Recipient SA-QE-RI KA: 30 (compound name?)
KU-RO          180     Total
```

**Assessment**: The commodity reading is preferred because:
1. Commodity+quantity is a standard Linear A pattern (NAME + COMMODITY + QUANTITY)
2. Having KA appear as both a standalone entry and suffix on names would be unusual
3. HT 140 shows KA functioning as a distinct commodity alongside OLE variants
4. The separator (𐄁) on Line 7 separates SA-QE-RI from KA, suggesting they are different elements

**Confidence**: Commodity reading PROBABLE; name reading POSSIBLE.

## Novel Observations

### 1. Largest Verified KU-RO Total

HT 11b's KU-RO = 180 is the largest arithmetically verified total in the corpus. This exceeds HT 13 (130.5 VIN, near-match) and HT 85a (66 VIR, verified). The scale suggests institutional-level commodity management.

### 2. KA as Unidentified Commodity Logogram

KA is not in the standard Linear B/Linear A commodity logogram inventory (GRA, VIN, OLE, OLIV, FIC, OVI, CAP, SUS, BOS, VIR, TELA, CYP). Its appearance as a commodity marker on HT 11b and HT 140 suggests either:
- A commodity not preserved in the Linear B administrative system
- A Minoan-specific category that Greeks did not continue recording
- A local product or measurement not standardized across sites

### 3. Decade-Unit Quantities

All five quantities (40, 30, 50, 30, 30) are multiples of 10. This is unique among analyzed tablets. Possible explanations:
- The commodity is counted in standard lots of 10 (like modern "cases" or "bales")
- The scribe rounded to the nearest 10 (approximation)
- The accounting operates at a scale where single-unit precision is unnecessary

### 4. Vocabulary Isolation

HT 11b contains no words shared with any other tablet (except KA and KU-RO). This is the most vocabulary-isolated tablet analyzed so far. Even its partner (HT 11a) shares no vocabulary. This suggests:
- A specialized administrative domain not connected to the major HT distribution networks
- Names associated with a particular commodity sector that does not overlap with wine (HT 9, 13), personnel (HT 85a, 122), or grain (HT 86a) networks

### 5. *86 Sign Connects HT and KH Administrative Systems

The *86 sign appears in both the Khania roundel system (*86+*188 stamps) and HT administrative texts (*86-KA on HT 11b, *86 on HT 94b, *86-SI-NI on HT 140). This cross-site presence of *86 is notable because the Khania system otherwise has minimal vocabulary overlap with HT (1.8%). The *86 sign may represent a commodity, institution, or concept that transcends site-specific administrative traditions.
