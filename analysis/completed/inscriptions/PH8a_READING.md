2. **Phaistos-unique logograms**: *416+L2 and *418+L2 are compound logograms not attested outside Phaistos. The +L2 suffix ligature links them as a pair (possibly two grades or types of the same commodity class).
3. **VIR+[?] with 11 persons**: 11 is a relatively large personnel count for Phaistos. For comparison, PH12a records 10 (VIR-*339-HIDE compound). This suggests Phaistos administered significant labor allocations.

6. **Damaged on both ends**: Lines 1 and 10 are both damaged (𐝫), meaning the tablet is incomplete. The original document was likely longer.
7. **No syllabographic words**: Zero word-signs. This means NO linguistic hypothesis testing is possible from the syllabographic content. The tablet's value is entirely structural and logographic.
8. **No KU-RO/KI-RO**: No totaling vocabulary. The sections may be self-contained allocations rather than components of a summation.

## Novel Observations

### 1. Phaistos-Unique Logogram Pairs with Shared Ligature Suffix

The *416+L2 / *418+L2 pair on PH8a is the first documented instance of two Phaistos-unique logograms sharing a ligature suffix (+L2). This parallels the Khania CYP grading system (CYP+D, CYP+E) and suggests that Phaistos had its own commodity classification system with qualifier suffixes. The +L2 element may encode quality grade, processing stage, or provenance -- a local administrative innovation not adopted at other sites.

### 2. Sectional Accounting Sophistication

PH8a's three-section structure with ruling dividers demonstrates that Phaistos scribes organized multi-category allocations with explicit visual separators. This is more sophisticated than simple sequential lists and approaches the complexity of HT multi-section tablets (e.g., HT123+124a's three-column structure). The Phaistos administrative system was not primitive despite being one of the earliest.

### 3. Pan-Minoan + Local Logogram Coexistence

PH8a simultaneously uses pan-Minoan signs (VIR, *304 -- attested at 5+ sites) and Phaistos-unique signs (*416+L2, *418+L2). This demonstrates that local administrative systems could innovate new logograms while retaining the shared Minoan sign inventory. The administrative vocabulary was not monolithic but layered: a shared pan-Minoan base with local extensions.

### 4. Logographic Purity

PH8a contains zero syllabographic words. While tablets like PH3a also lack multi-syllable words, PH8a is distinctive in being a full-sized sectional tablet (not a small inventory) that is entirely logographic. This suggests that some Phaistos accounting operations were conducted entirely in the logographic register, without syllabographic names or labels.
