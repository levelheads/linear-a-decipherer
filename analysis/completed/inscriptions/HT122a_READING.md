### Notable Structural Features

1. **Grand total verification**: 31 + 65 + 1 = 97 = PO-TO-KU-RO. VERIFIED.
2. **Two-section document**: Different headers (RA-RI vs. JE-DI) suggest two institutional contributors.
3. **Shared scribe**: Both sides written by HT Scribe 9, indicating simultaneous record.
4. **Size asymmetry**: Side a has ~15 recipients totaling 31; side b has 5 recipients totaling 65. Side b has far fewer but much larger allocations.
5. **VIR commodity**: VIR+[?] appears only on side b header, but the commodity applies to both sides (whole-number allocations, no fractions, PO-TO-KU-RO encompasses both).
6. **KU-PA₃-NU duplicate**: Unique feature -- same name appears twice on side a (Lines 12 and 16).
7. **Post-total KU-DA**: Rare position after KU-RO. Included in PO-TO-KU-RO grand total, suggesting it is part of the accounting but added after the section was initially totaled.
8. **Damage pattern**: Concentrated in Lines 1-4 (opening). The 11-unit gap (31 - 20 = 11) represents lost entries in this damaged section.
9. **Scale comparison**: HT 85a (same scribe) totals 66 VIR under A-DU; HT 122 totals 97 VIR under RA-RI + JE-DI. These are the two largest known VIR documents at HT.
10. **Same scribe as HT 85a**: Both HT 85a and HT 122 are by HT Scribe 9 -- the "personnel specialist" at Hagia Triada.

## What We Know For Certain

1. **Document function**: This is a two-section personnel allocation document. RA-RI and JE-DI are institutional sources; named individuals receive worker allocations; PO-TO-KU-RO sums the grand total.

4. **PO-TO-KU-RO function**: PO-TO-KU-RO marks the grand total across all sections and addenda. CERTAIN.

5. **VIR commodity**: The document records personnel/workers, confirmed by VIR+[?] logogram on side b and the integer-only quantities throughout.

7. **HT Scribe 9 wrote both HT 85a and HT 122**: This scribe specializes in personnel (VIR) records.

## What We Hypothesize

1. **RA-RI and JE-DI are institutional names/sources**: By analogy with A-DU (contributor) on HT 85a, RA-RI and JE-DI likely name the administrative units providing the workers. RA-RI is a hapax; JE-DI appears only on HT 122b.

2. **KU-DA as a post-total addendum**: KU-DA 1 appears after the KU-RO 31 total but is included in PO-TO-KU-RO 97. This may be a late addition, a correction, or a separate category of worker. Its exact function is uncertain.

3. **The flat distribution on side a reflects a different administrative pattern**: Unlike HT 85a (where DA-SI-*118 alone gets 36%), side a of HT 122a shows most recipients getting 1 unit. This may indicate maintenance allocations, supplementary workers, or a different type of labor assignment.

4. **KU-PA₃-NU's double listing**: Whether this represents two people, two capacities, or an error is unknown. The duplicate is unusual but not impossible in Minoan administration.

5. **The HT 9 cohort (PA-DE, *306-TU, *324-DI-RA)**: These three individuals appear together on HT 9a/9b (wine) and HT 122a (workers). They may represent a functional unit (department, estate, or office) that receives both commodity rations and personnel allocations.

6. **Damage accounts for 11 missing units on side a**: The opening lacuna (Lines 1-4) likely contained entries summing to approximately 11 (31 - 20 = 11), plus PA's lost quantity.

7. **Side b has more severe damage**: Only 15 of 65 units are accounted for on side b (23% computable). The remaining 50 units are from lost entries.

## Novel Observations

### 1. The Grand Total Equation as Arithmetic Proof

The equation 31 + 65 + 1 = 97 = PO-TO-KU-RO provides the strongest evidence yet for how PO-TO-KU-RO relates to KU-RO. PO-TO-KU-RO is not merely the sum of two KU-RO values (31 + 65 = 96), but encompasses ALL items in the document including post-total addenda. This has implications for understanding the Minoan accounting system: scribes tracked grand totals that included corrections and late entries.

### 2. HT Scribe 9 as Personnel Specialist

HT Scribe 9 authored both HT 85a (66 VIR under A-DU) and HT 122 (97 VIR under RA-RI + JE-DI). These are the two largest known VIR distribution documents at Hagia Triada. This scribe appears to have specialized in personnel accounting, handling records involving 163+ workers total across just two tablets. This suggests functional specialization among Minoan scribes.

### 3. The HT 9 Cohort Across Commodity Types

PA-DE, *306-TU, and *324-DI-RA form a cohort that receives wine rations (HT 9a/9b) and personnel allocations (HT 122a). This cross-commodity linkage strongly suggests these names refer to institutional positions, estates, or departments -- not just individual persons -- since such entities would receive both commodity rations and labor allocations.

### 4. Flat Distribution Pattern

Unlike HT 85a's concentrated distribution (DA-SI-*118 = 36%), HT 122a side a shows a remarkably flat pattern: 10 of 14 computable recipients receive exactly 1 unit. This may indicate:
- **Maintenance allocations**: Each recipient gets a minimal staff complement
- **Supplementary assignments**: Additional workers on top of a baseline not recorded here
- **Specialized workers**: The VIR+[?] qualifier may denote a specific worker type distributed in small numbers

### 5. DA-SI-*118's Reduced Allocation

DA-SI-*118 receives only 2 workers on HT 122a, compared to 24 workers on HT 85a (from A-DU). This dramatic difference (2 vs. 24) between documents from the same scribe may reflect: different types of workers (VIR+[?] vs. VIR), different time periods, different institutional sources (RA-RI vs. A-DU), or supplementary versus primary allocations.

### 6. The U-DE-ZA Link Between Sections

U-DE-ZA appears on side a (Line 1, as header qualifier or recipient) and is embedded in A-RA-JU-U-DE-ZA on side b. This name-sharing is notable. A-RA-JU-U-DE-ZA may be a compound name meaning "[X]-of-U-DE-ZA" or a descriptive identifier linking to the entity U-DE-ZA from section 1. This cross-section link reinforces the document's internal coherence.
