## Structural Analysis

### Document Type

**Compact distribution list with VERIFIED KU-RO and unspecified commodity**

HT 104 is a structurally simple but arithmetically important tablet: 3 recipients receive quantities summing exactly to KU-RO = 95.

### Document Structure

```
[H]   TA-PA  𐄁                       Header: institutional source
[?]   TE+RO[  𐄁                      Commodity or modifier (damaged)
[R]   DA-KU-SE-NE-TI     45  ¹⁄₂     Recipient 1: 45.5 (LARGEST, 47.9%)
[R]   I-DU-TI             20  ¹⁄₂     Recipient 2: 20.5 (21.6%)
[R]   PA-DA-SU-TI         29          Recipient 3: 29 (30.5%)
[T]   KU-RO               95          TOTAL (VERIFIED)
```

### Notable Structural Features

1. **All vocabulary hapax**: Every word on this tablet (except KU-RO) appears nowhere else in the corpus. This complete vocabulary isolation makes cross-tablet verification impossible.

2. **-TI suffix on all three recipients**: DA-KU-SE-NE-**TI**, I-DU-**TI**, PA-DA-SU-**TI**. The shared suffix is striking and strongly suggests a morphological pattern:
   - In Luwian, -TI is a verbal ending (3sg present) or nominal suffix
   - The -TI ending could indicate: place names (locatives), professional titles, or clan/group affiliations
   - The pattern X-TI = "the one of X" or "X-place" is compatible with Anatolian naming

3. **Polysyllabic names**: DA-KU-SE-NE-TI (5 syllables) and PA-DA-SU-TI (4 syllables) are among the longest words on any HT tablet. Long words favor agglutinative languages (Luwian, Hurrian).

4. **Large quantities**: 45.5, 20.5, and 29 are substantial allocations. The total (95) is comparable to HT 122 (PO-TO-KU-RO 97) and suggests this records a significant administrative operation.

5. **TE+RO[ is damaged**: The "[" notation indicates the sign is broken. TE+RO- may be the beginning of a compound logogram or commodity name. If completed as TE-RO or similar, it could indicate a specific commodity type.

6. **Fractions resolve**: ¹⁄₂ + ¹⁄₂ = 1, so the fractional entries produce a whole-number total (95). This demonstrates scribal arithmetic competence with fractions.

7. **HT Scribe 5**: This scribe is otherwise uncharacterized in our readings. Casa Room 7 findspot is shared with HT 86a/86b (Scribe 6) and HT 88 (Scribe 7).

## Novel Observations

### 1. -TI Suffix as Morphological Pattern

The uniform -TI ending across all three recipients is the strongest morphological pattern on any single tablet. If -TI represents a Luwian locative or verbal suffix, the three names may be place names or administrative districts: "X-place," "Y-place," "Z-place." This would make HT 104 a geographic allocation record.

### 2. Complete Vocabulary Isolation

HT 104 shares NO vocabulary with any other tablet except KU-RO. This level of isolation is rare -- even HT 11b (largely isolated) shared KA with common usage. TA-PA, DA-KU-SE-NE-TI, I-DU-TI, and PA-DA-SU-TI are all hapax legomena. This may indicate a specialized administrative context or a transaction involving entities not otherwise recorded at Hagia Triada.

### 3. 8th Verified KU-RO

HT 104 adds to the verified KU-RO inventory (now 8 VERIFIED + 4 CONSTRAINED). The 95-unit total is the third-largest verified total after HT 11b (180) and HT 122 (PO-TO-KU-RO 97).
