## Structural Analysis

### Document Type

**Multi-section personnel roster (Schoep Type IV)**

This tablet does NOT record commodity distributions. It records persons, each allocated exactly 1 unit of an unstated commodity or entitlement. The three-section structure suggests organizational categories (workplace, department, or origin).

### Two-Section Structure of Side a (Focus)

**Section 1** (a.1-6): The primary list, with a three-element header and a KU-RO total.

```
HEADER:  MA-KA-RI-TE | KI-RO | U-MI-NA-SI
LIST:    10 personal names, each followed by numeral 1
TOTAL:   KU-RO 10
```

**Section 2** (a.7-9): A secondary list, introduced by SA-TA, with no KU-RO.

```
HEADER:  SA-TA
LIST:    5 personal names, each followed by numeral 1
TOTAL:   [NONE]
```

**Rule line** between sections (after a.6 / before a.7): A physical horizontal incision separating the two accounting units. This is the scribe's deliberate demarcation -- the same convention observed on other HT tablets.

Its appearance here as a personal name raises the question: is KI-DA-RO an extended form of the K-R root used as a name, or simply a personal name with coincidental phonological similarity? ## Novel Observations

### 1. KI-RO in Header Position: Organizational Marker

HT 117a provides definitive evidence that KI-RO is NOT exclusively a "deficit" marker. Here it appears in the tripartite header (MA-KA-RI-TE | KI-RO | U-MI-NA-SI) BEFORE any list entries, separated by word divider dots. This is a categorical/organizational use.

**Implication**: The K-R paradigm is more complex than "KU-RO = total, KI-RO = deficit." The U/I vowel alternation may encode a broader semantic opposition (complete/partial, received/outstanding, present/absent) rather than a simple total/deficit pair.

### 2. Both KI-RO and KU-RO on One Tablet

HT 117a contains BOTH KI-RO (header, a.1) and KU-RO (total, a.6). This co-occurrence is significant because:
- It proves these are NOT alternative forms for the same function
- They occupy different structural slots (header vs. totaling line)
- The K-R vowel alternation paradigm is confirmed as a multi-term system

Only a few tablets contain both terms. HT 94 is another (KU-RO in a.3, KI-RO in b.1). This co-occurrence pattern strengthens the K-R paradigm hypothesis.

### 3. Dual-Section Structure Without Dual Totals

Section 1 has KU-RO 10. Section 2 has no total. This asymmetry is deliberate -- the scribe (HT Scribe 9) clearly knew how to write totals. Possible explanations:
- Section 2 is a sub-category that does not warrant its own total
- Section 2 is ongoing/incomplete
- Section 2 is a different accounting type (e.g., seconded personnel vs. permanent)
- The tablet was not yet finished (side b has only 2 names + vacant space)

### 4. Every Recipient Gets Exactly 1 Unit

This uniform allocation (every name followed by 1) is distinctive. Most HT commodity lists show variable quantities (e.g., HT 13: quantities range from 1 to 57; HT 28: quantities range from 1 to 20). The uniformity here suggests:
- **Headcount** (pure census/roster function)
- **Equal ration** (each person receives one unit of something unstated)
- **Labor allocation** (each person = 1 work unit)

The absence of commodity logograms makes "equal ration" less likely (no stated commodity). "Headcount" or "labor allocation" better fits the evidence.

### 5. Scribe 9 Consistency (HT 85a Connection)

HT Scribe 9 wrote both HT 85a and HT 117a. Both tablets feature:
- Exact KU-RO arithmetic verification (66 in HT 85a, 10 in HT 117a)
- Clear section structure
- Personnel-focused content

This is our first opportunity for intra-scribe comparison. Scribe 9's accounting practice appears meticulous -- both totals verify exactly, suggesting this scribe (or the scribal training at Hagia Triada) maintained high computational accuracy.

### 6. SA-TA Section Has No KU-RO

The absence of a total for the SA-TA section may indicate:
- SA-TA introduces an addendum or secondary category
- The 5 names under SA-TA may be additions to the primary roster
- SA-TA may represent a different institutional context where totaling is not required

### 7. 15 Personal Names: Onomastic Density

With 17 total names across all three sections (10 + 5 + 2), HT 117 is one of the richest onomastic sources in the HT corpus. The name distribution shows:
- **Length range**: 2 syllables (U-SU, MI-TU, MA-RU) to 5 syllables (MI-RU-TA-RA-RE)
- **Reduplication**: KU-KU-DA-RA (Section 2)
- **Prefix patterns**: KU- (3 names), DA- (2 names), U- (2 names)
- **No obvious Greek morphology**: Confirms non-Greek onomastic tradition
