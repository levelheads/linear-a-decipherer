## Structural Analysis

### Document Type

**Mixed commodity account tablet (Schoep Type Ia)**

This tablet records multiple commodity types under a primary heading (A-SI-JA-KA), with individual allocations to named persons.

### Document Structure

**Side A:**
1. **Header**: A-SI-JA-KA (account holder/heading)
2. **First entry**: JA-QI(f) - GRA+QE 5
3. **Commodity block 1**: Oil variants (OLE+U, OLE+KI, OLE+MI, OLE+TU)
4. **Allocation marker**: SA-RA2 OLE+DI 1
5. **Additional commodities**: FIC 2, VIN 3
6. **Personnel-commodity**: VIR+KA VIN 6 (6 workers associated with wine)
7. **Named allocation**: A-RU-DA-RA - GRA 5, *304 2
8. **Oil entries**: OLE+DI 3
9. **Named allocation**: I-TA-JA - OLE+DI 10

**Side B:**
1. **Expanded header**: A-SI-JA-KA + U-MI-NA-SI
2. **Allocation marker**: SA-RA2 GRA 20
3. **Commodity list**: OLE+DI 5, FIC 2, VIN 4
4. **Named allocation**: PU-RA2 - FIC 6
5. **Named allocation**: JA-QI(f) - VIN 6
6. **Named allocation**: WI-DI-NA - OLE+DI 3, VIN 3
7. **Final fraction**: E (1/2)

### Notable Structural Features

1. **Opisthograph**: Both sides inscribed and related
2. **No KU-RO**: Unlike HT 13, this tablet lacks a totaling line
3. **SA-RA2 usage**: Appears twice as allocation marker
4. **Multiple oil types**: OLE+U, OLE+KI, OLE+MI, OLE+TU, OLE+DI - indicating differentiated olive oil products
5. **VIR+KA notation**: Personnel associated with wine transport/handling
6. **Personal names end in -A**: Possible feminine names (Godart 1984; Schoep 2002)

## Novel Observations

### 1. Oil Type Differentiation

HT 28 provides the richest single-tablet evidence for olive oil type differentiation:
- OLE+U, OLE+KI, OLE+MI, OLE+TU, OLE+DI (5 types)

**Hypothesis**: The syllabographic element indicates processing stage, quality grade, or intended use. OLE+DI is most common, possibly indicating "standard" oil.

### 2. SA-RA2 as Allocation Section Marker

SA-RA2 appears to introduce allocation sections within an account, rather than functioning as a standalone entry. This is consistent with Akkadian *saraku* "to allocate, grant" and supports the Semitic administrative vocabulary layer hypothesis.

### 3. Dual-Function Terminology

U-MI-NA-SI appears in both:
- Administrative context (HT 28b header)
- Religious context (peak sanctuary libation formulas)

This may indicate:
- Priestly involvement in palatial administration
- Religious legitimation of economic transactions
- Dual meaning of term applicable to both domains
