## Structural Analysis

### HT 86b as Partial Duplicate of HT 86a Section 1

HT 86b is nearly identical to Section 1 of HT 86a:

| Feature | HT 86a Section 1 | HT 86b |
|---------|------------------|--------|
| Header | A-KA-RU 𐄁 | A-KA-RU 𐄁 |
| Commodity | GRA+K+L | GRA+K+L |
| Entry 1 | KU-NI-SU 20 | KU-NI-SU GRA+K+L 20 |
| Entry 2 | SA-RU 20 | SA-RU 20 |
| Entry 3 | DI-DE-RU 20 | DI-DE (truncated) |
| Entry 4 | QA-RA₂-WA 10 | [absent] |

**Key observations**:

1. **Identical header and first two entries**: A-KA-RU header, KU-NI-SU 20, SA-RU 20 -- word-for-word match with side a Section 1.

2. **DI-DE is truncated**: The third entry begins DI-DE but lacks the final -RU syllable. This could be:
   - Tablet damage (the scribe wrote DI-DE-RU but the end broke off)
   - An abbreviation (DI-DE as short form of DI-DE-RU)
   - A different word (DI-DE as distinct from DI-DE-RU)
   Given that DI-DE appears only here (hapax) while DI-DE-RU appears on 3 tablets (all GRA cohort), truncation/damage is most likely.

3. **GRA+K+L repeated**: On side a, GRA+K+L appears in the header. On side b, GRA+K+L appears explicitly with KU-NI-SU's entry (Line 2). This redundant commodity marking may indicate side b is a separate version or draft, not a continuation.

4. **QA-RA₂-WA and Section 2 absent**: Side b lacks the fourth recipient (QA-RA₂-WA) and the entire A-DU Section 2 (DA-ME, MI-NU-TE). This may be due to tablet breakage or because side b was a partial record.

## Novel Observations

### 1. Administrative Duplication

HT 86b nearly duplicates HT 86a Section 1. This is the clearest evidence of administrative duplication in the corpus. Possible explanations:
- **Draft and final**: Side b is a preliminary version; side a is the complete record
- **Running update**: Side b records an earlier state; side a adds Section 2 (A-DU)
- **Parallel record**: Both sides track the same distribution for different administrative purposes

### 2. GRA+K+L Explicit on Entry Line

On side b, GRA+K+L appears explicitly with KU-NI-SU's entry (Line 2), whereas on side a it appears only in the header. This suggests the scribe wrote side b as a standalone record (commodity per entry), then reorganized side a to use header-level commodity declaration (commodity in header, entries inherit).
