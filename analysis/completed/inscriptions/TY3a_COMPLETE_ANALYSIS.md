## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

TY 3a is one of the largest Linear A tablets from Tylissos, containing a detailed oil distribution record with:

- **68 entries** - second longest tablet inscription
- **Multiple oil types** - OLE+KI, OLE+U, OLE+MI, OLE+QIf, OLE+NE, OLE+TU
- **Named recipients** - ZA-*321, NE-KI, A-DU, A-DA, KO-A-DU-WA, A-KU-TU-*361
- **Precise quantities** - integers plus fractions (¬π‚ÅÑ‚ÇÇ, ¬π‚ÅÑ‚ÇÑ, ¬π‚ÅÑ‚ÇÖ)
- **Sectional organization** - horizontal dividers separate accounting sections
- **Administrative function** - oil distribution from central storage

**Key Finding**: The tablet demonstrates the sophisticated administrative apparatus at Tylissos, with differentiated oil products and precise fractional accounting - matching the complexity found at larger centers like Hagia Triada.

## PART 10: CONCLUSIONS

### 10.1 Key Findings

1. **Administrative Sophistication**: TY 3a demonstrates that Tylissos operated a sophisticated oil distribution system comparable to larger centers.

2. **Oil Differentiation**: Eight distinct oil types/grades were tracked, suggesting specialized production and distribution.

3. **Mixed Linguistic Elements**:
   - Semitic influence in administrative terms (A-DU, A-DA)
   - Luwian influence in personal names (KO-A-DU-WA)
   - Pre-Greek substrate in some vocabulary (NE-KI)

4. **No Totaling Function**: Unlike many HT tablets, TY 3a lacks KU-RO, suggesting it records distributions, not summaries.

5. **Fractional Precision**: The use of ¬π‚ÅÑ‚ÇÖ fractions indicates a decimal-compatible system alongside the common ¬π‚ÅÑ‚ÇÇ and ¬π‚ÅÑ‚ÇÑ.

2. **Contact Language Evidence**: The mixing of Semitic and Luwian elements supports a multilingual administrative elite.

3. **Economic Network**: The detailed oil accounting suggests integration into a broader Cretan trade network.
