## Integration with Track C Phase C1

### Khania Administrative System

Based on KH 5 and KH 88, the Khania system:

1. **Does not use K-R vocabulary** - confirmed across both tablets
2. **Uses copper grade modifiers** (CYP+E in KH 5) - specialized accounting
3. **Employs compound signs** (*301-NA, *21F-*118) - local vocabulary
4. **Tracks mixed commodities per person** - allocation-centric, not commodity-centric
5. **Heavy fraction usage** - more precise measurement than HT

### Hypothesis: Why No K-R Vocabulary?

| Hypothesis | Evidence from KH 5/88 | Score |
|------------|----------------------|-------|
| **Different function** (export vs distribution) | Mixed personal allocations | HIGH |
| **Different scribal tradition** | Unique compound signs | MEDIUM |
| **Chronological difference** | Both LMIB (same as HT) | LOW |
| **Geographic independence** | Distinct personnel names | MEDIUM |

**Leading interpretation**: Khania handled personal allocations and exports; HT handled centralized redistribution. Different functions = different vocabulary.
