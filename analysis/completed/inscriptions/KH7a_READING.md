2. **CYP+E with ¹⁄₁₆**: The fraction ¹⁄₁₆ with the supposedly "higher-grade" marker COMPLICATES the simple hypothesis. CYP+E = higher grade is maintained, but the integer/fraction dichotomy is weakened.

## Novel Observations

### 1. CYP Grading System: Tendency, Not Rule

KH7a forces a revision of the CYP grading hypothesis. CYP+D with integer (1) and CYP+E with fraction (¹⁄₁₆) demonstrate that the grade markers indicate copper quality (purity, refinement level, or ore type), while quantities can be either integer or fractional regardless of grade. The statistical tendency (CYP+D usually fractional, CYP+E usually integer) may reflect typical transaction sizes: lower-grade copper is commonly traded in fractional units, while higher-grade copper is typically traded in whole units. But both grades can accommodate either format.

### 2. Mixed Copper + Labor: Integrated Resource Planning

KH7a is the first analyzed Khania tablet showing copper allocations integrated with workforce assignments (VIR+*313b). This suggests Khania's administrative system tracked not just copper quantities but the labor needed to process them. The three labor entries (10, 4, 18 = 32 workers) associated with four CYP+D entries implies a copper-to-labor ratio was being managed. This is evidence for a more sophisticated administrative apparatus at Khania than simple commodity ledgers.

### 3. Domain Layering at Khania

KH7a provides the first clear domain-layering evidence within a single Khania tablet:
- **QA-TI-KI** (Luwian SUPPORTED, 2.0) — personal name / functional title
- **KO-E** (Semitic SUPPORTED, 7.7) — possibly administrative/supervisory term

This mirrors the corpus-wide pattern (Luwian morphology for names/religious terms, Semitic for administrative vocabulary) but extends it to Khania's parallel system. The domain layering is not HT-specific — it is pan-Minoan.

### 4. KO-E: Strongest Semitic Signal at Khania

KO-E's Semitic score (7.7) is the highest for any term on an analyzed KH tablet. It commands the largest labor unit (18 workers). If KO-E is a Semitic loan word designating an administrative role (overseer, allocator), this parallels the Semitic administrative vocabulary at HT (A-DU, KU-RO, SA-RA₂). Even Khania, with its independent CYP-based system, borrowed Semitic administrative terms.

### 5. VIR+*313b as KH Labor Logogram

The VIR+*313b compound is a distinctive Khania personnel marker. Three occurrences with integer quantities (10, 4, 18) establish it as a workforce counting unit. At HT, VIR alone designates personnel. The +*313b qualifier at Khania may specify a particular worker category (metallurgical laborers? copper smiths?), consistent with Khania's specialization in copper administration.

### 6. CYP+E ¹⁄₁₆: Precision Measurement

The fraction ¹⁄₁₆ with CYP+E is the smallest copper fraction attested at Khania (also the smallest fraction seen elsewhere, on SAMWa1). If CYP+E = higher-grade copper, then ¹⁄₁₆ of a unit of high-grade copper may represent a small but precise allocation — perhaps for a specialized purpose (alloying, finishing, ritual?). The precision to 6.25% of a unit demonstrates sophisticated measurement capability.
