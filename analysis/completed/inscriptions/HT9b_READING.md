## The Opisthograph Relationship: HT 9a / HT 9b

### This is the most significant structural feature of this tablet.

Both sides of HT 9 share:
1. **The same header system**: PA₃ | WA-JA-PI (side b) corresponds to the position after KU-RO on side a
2. **The same seven recipients**: PA-DE, *306-TU, DI-NA-U, QE-PU, *324-DI-RA, TA-I-AROM/TA-I-*123, plus A-RU (side a only)
3. **Independent totals**: Each side has its own KU-RO

### Side-by-Side Comparison

| Recipient | HT 9a (Side A) | HT 9b (Side B) | Difference | Direction |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|-----------|
| PA-DE | 5.75 | 3 | -2.75 | Decrease |
| *306-TU | 10 | 8 | -2 | Decrease |
| DI-NA-U | 4 | 4 | 0 | Same |
| QE-PU | 2 | 2 | 0 | Same |
| *324-DI-RA | 2.5 | 2 | -0.5 | Decrease |
| TA-I-AROM | 2.5 | 2 | -0.5 | Decrease |
| A-RU | 4.25 | (absent) | -4.25 | Absent |
| **KU-RO** | **31.75** | **24** | **-7.75** | Decrease |

### Key Observations

1. **Side b quantities are always less than or equal to side a** -- no recipient gets MORE on side b
2. **DI-NA-U and QE-PU are identical on both sides** (4 and 2 respectively)
3. **A-RU appears only on side a** -- this name is completely absent from side b
4. **Side b has no fractions** -- all quantities are whole integers, while side a uses J and E fractions extensively
5. **The commodity (VIN = wine)** is stated only in side a's header (SA-RO | TE | VIN); side b omits the commodity entirely

### Structural Interpretation

The relationship between sides a and b is not random. The consistent pattern of side b <= side a for every recipient strongly suggests one of the following:

**Hypothesis A: Double-Entry Accounting** -- Side a records the full allocation (what is owed), side b records what was actually disbursed. The difference represents outstanding balances.

**Hypothesis B: Two Periods** -- Side a records allocations from one period (with fractional adjustments), side b records allocations from a second period (round numbers only).

**Hypothesis C: Correction/Adjustment** -- Side b is a corrected or adjusted version of side a. Younger's suggestion that PA₃ might denote a "correction" (citing Davis's interpretations of HT 8) supports this.

**Our Assessment**: Hypothesis A (double-entry accounting) best explains the data because:
- The quantities decrease uniformly (never increase)
- The whole-number nature of side b suggests it records actual physical disbursements
- The fractional nature of side a suggests it records calculated entitlements
- A-RU's absence from side b could mean this recipient received nothing in the actual disbursement

**Confidence**: POSSIBLE (structural pattern is clear, but functional interpretation remains uncertain)

## Novel Observations

### 1. Double-Entry Accounting Pattern

HT 9 provides the clearest evidence in the Linear A corpus for **double-entry or dual-record accounting**. The same seven recipients appear on both sides with different (but systematically related) quantities. Side b values are always <= side a values, with no exceptions. This is not random variation; it is a structured administrative relationship.

**Significance**: If confirmed across other opisthographic tablets, this would demonstrate that Minoan scribes practiced a form of double-entry bookkeeping approximately 3,000 years before Luca Pacioli's 1494 treatise (though not in the modern sense of debits and credits).

**Comparable tablets**: HT 28 (opisthograph with related sides), ZA 4 (double-sided accounting without explicit totals), TY 3 (double-sided with debit pattern).

### 2. Whole-Number vs. Fractional Distribution

Side a uses fractions extensively (J and E), while side b uses only whole integers. This dichotomy within a single tablet may indicate:
- Side a = theoretical calculation of entitlements (allowing fractions)
- Side b = actual physical disbursement (rounded to whole units)

This has implications for understanding Linear A fraction signs: they may represent calculated amounts rather than measured quantities.

### 3. A-RU: The Missing Recipient

A-RU appears on side a (4.25 units) but is entirely absent from side b. If our double-entry hypothesis is correct, A-RU received no actual disbursement despite being allocated 4.25 units. This is the only recipient eliminated between sides -- all six other recipients appear on both.

### 4. *306-TU as Largest Recipient

*306-TU receives the largest allocation on both sides (10 on side a, 8 on side b). This consistent prominence suggests either a larger household, a more important official, or a location with greater need. The name contains undeciphered sign *306, preventing etymological analysis.

### 5. AROM in a Wine Context

If TA-I-AROM is indeed TA-I + the AROM logogram (herbs/spices), its appearance in a wine distribution list is striking. This could indicate:
- TA-I is a recipient who receives aromatics alongside wine
- AROM functions as a phonetic sign within the name, not logographically
- The distribution includes both wine and aromatic herbs (mixed commodity)

The presence on HT 39.1 (TA-I-*123) in a different context may help disambiguate.

### 6. SA-RO as S-R Variant

Side a's header begins with SA-RO, which belongs to the S-R consonant skeleton pattern documented in KNOWLEDGE.md (SA-RA₂, SA-RU, SA-RO, SI-RU). SA-RO has only 4 attestations, all at HT. In this context, SA-RO precedes TE and VIN, functioning as a heading element -- possibly "allocation [of/from]" consistent with the S-R paradigm's proposed meaning of administrative allocation.

### 7. Arithmetic Verification Asymmetry

HT 9 now provides a unique case study: one side (b) verifies perfectly, while the other (a) has a systematic discrepancy of exactly J+E (0.75). This asymmetry within a single tablet is methodologically valuable -- it rules out blanket explanations (like "the scribe made arithmetic errors" or "we misunderstand the fraction system") and suggests the discrepancy is intentional or structural.
