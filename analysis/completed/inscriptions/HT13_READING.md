| **Stated KU-RO** | **130 J (= 130.5)** |

**RESULT: MISMATCH** -- Computed 131.0, stated 130.5. Difference = -0.5 (computed EXCEEDS stated KU-RO by 0.5).

**Diagnosis**: fraction_parsing (arithmetic_verifier). The computed sum exceeds the stated KU-RO value by exactly one J (1/2 unit). This is the OPPOSITE pattern from HT 9a, where the stated total exceeds the computed sum.

**Possible Explanations**:
1. **Scribal arithmetic**: The scribe may have miscounted one of the fractions
2. **Fraction loss in KU-RO**: The KU-RO value may have been written as 130 J but intended as 131, with a fraction sign missed or damaged
3. **Parser overcounting**: One of the J fractions may have been incorrectly parsed -- e.g., if RE-ZA's quantity is 5 (not 5 J), the sum would be 130.5, matching KU-RO exactly
4. **Intentional adjustment**: The total may deliberately exclude half a unit (administrative rounding, partial disbursement, or tax/tithe deduction)

**Methodological Note**: This is an unusually small discrepancy (0.38% of total) for such a large distribution. The near-perfect match (130.5 vs. 131) strongly supports the reading that all 6 entries are correctly identified and KU-RO functions as total.

## Novel Observations

### 1. Largest VIN Distribution in Reading Corpus

HT 13 records 130.5 units of wine, dwarfing HT 9a (31.75). The scale difference (4x larger) suggests HT 13 may represent a different administrative level -- estate-level or institutional distribution rather than individual household allocation.

### 2. Extreme Concentration: TE-TU at 43%

TE-TU's 56 units (43% of total) is the most concentrated single-recipient allocation in our reading corpus. On HT 85a, DA-SI-*118 receives 36% (24/66), but that is VIR (personnel). A 43% concentration of wine to one recipient is anomalous and suggests institutional rather than individual allocation. If TE-TU is a temple, palace workshop, or major estate, the scale becomes comprehensible.

### 3. Reversed Arithmetic Discrepancy

HT 9a: computed (31) < stated (31.75) -- total EXCEEDS sum by 0.75
HT 13: computed (131) > stated (130.5) -- sum EXCEEDS total by 0.5

These opposite-direction discrepancies across two VIN distribution tablets are methodologically valuable. They suggest the discrepancies are not systematic parser bias (which would always err in one direction) but rather reflect real variation in scribal practice, tablet damage, or our fraction parsing.

### 4. Zero Recipient Overlap with HT 9a

Despite both being VIN distribution tablets at HT, HT 13 and HT 9a share ZERO recipients. This is striking -- the two wine distribution records involve completely different sets of people/places. Possible explanations:
- Different administrative districts within HT
- Different time periods (both LMIB, but possibly different seasons/years)
- Different institutional authorities (KA-U-DE-TA vs. SA-RO)
- HT 13's recipients are at a higher administrative level (scale 4x larger)

### 5. DA-SI-*118: Cross-Tablet Major Recipient

DA-SI-*118 appears on 4 tablets with consistently large allocations (19 VIN, 24 VIR, 2 on HT 122a). This name contains undeciphered *118, preventing etymological analysis, but the administrative profile is clear: DA-SI-*118 is one of the most important recipients in the HT system. The name also appears on HT 85a (Scribe 9) and HT 13 (Scribe 8), confirming it is not scribe-specific notation.

### 6. HT Scribe 8 Attribution

HT 13 is attributed to HT Scribe 8. Scribe identification allows us to compare administrative conventions:
- Scribe 8 (HT 13): KA-U-DE-TA VIN | TE | header format; fractions in some entries; large quantities
- Scribe 9 (HT 85a): A-DU | *307+*387 | VIR+[?] header format; no fractions; medium quantities
- Unknown scribe (HT 9a/b): SA-RO | TE | VIN header; extensive fractions; smaller quantities

The header format variation across scribes (same TE separator, different leading terms) may reflect personal convention or different administrative functions.

### 7. Multi-Commodity Recipients as Toponym Evidence

RE-ZA appears with VIN (HT 13), VIR (HT 88), and CYP (KH 86). KU-ZU-NI appears with VIN (HT 13) and VIR (HT 85a). DA-SI-*118 appears with VIN (HT 13) and VIR (HT 85a). These multi-commodity appearances strongly favor toponym interpretation over personal name -- places receive various types of allocations while individuals are more likely to be commodity-specific.
