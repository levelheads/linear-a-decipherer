## Structural Analysis

### Document Type

**Oil commodity label on stone object (vessel/weight)**

PEZg5 is a minimal inscription on a stone object from Petras recording 1½ units of olive oil (OLE) with a qualifier (*307). This is the simplest possible administrative format: COMMODITY + QUALIFIER + QUANTITY.

### Document Structure

```
[C]  OLE                      Commodity: olive oil
[?]  *307                     Unknown qualifier/variant
[#]  1 ½                      Quantity: 1.5 units
```

| Tag | Role | Count |
|-----|------|-------|

| [?] | Unknown | 1 (*307) |
| [#] | Quantity | 2 (1 + ½) |

### Notable Structural Features

1. **Minimal format**: Only 4 elements. Among the shortest administrative inscriptions in the corpus.
2. **Stone support**: Inscribed on stone, not clay -- suggests a label, seal, or weight rather than a disposable record.
3. **No KU-RO/KI-RO**: No total or deficit marker. Consistent with a single-entry label.
4. **No personal names**: No header (A-DU), no recipients. This is a pure commodity notation.
5. **Fraction present**: The ½ indicates fractional accounting at Petras, consistent with corpus-wide practices.
6. **\*307 qualifier**: Appears between OLE and the numeral. This position is consistent with oil variant markers (compare OLE+U, OLE+KI at HT/TY).

### Cross-Reference: *307 in Corpus

*307 appears elsewhere:
- **HT 85a**: *307+*387 in header position (unknown function)
- **Commodity candidate**: *307+*387 is a CANDIDATE anchor for VIR (100% specificity, n=2)

On PEZg5, *307 appears alone (without *387) and in a different context (OLE commodity, not VIR). This suggests *307 may have a general qualifier function that combines differently depending on commodity context.

## Novel Observations

### 1. Petras Oil Economy Evidence

PEZg5 is the first Petras inscription in our connected reading series. Eastern Cretan Petras is an important Neopalatial center, and this oil inscription (on stone) provides evidence of oil commodity management at the site. Petras joins HT, TY, and KH as sites with documented oil administration.

### 2. Stone Object as Commodity Label

The stone support distinguishes PEZg5 from clay administrative tablets. Stone inscriptions tend to be more permanent and are often associated with vessels, weights, or ritual objects. An oil quantity label on stone may indicate:
- A vessel label (indicating contents)
- A weight or measure (indicating standard unit)
- A votive/dedicatory record

### 3. *307 as Cross-Context Qualifier

*307 appears in at least two different commodity contexts: with OLE (PEZg5) and with VIR as *307+*387 (HT 85a). If *307 functions as a general qualifier, this cross-commodity use mirrors how individual signs function in the OLE variant system (where +U, +KI, etc. each modify different base commodities in some contexts). However, with only 2 attestation contexts, this observation is preliminary.
