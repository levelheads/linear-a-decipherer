## Novel Observations

### 1. KU-PA Cross-Site Anchor Expansion

KU-PA's appearance with GRA+PA on ZA11a is the first verification at Zakros, expanding the anchor from 2 sites (HT, KH) to 3. This strengthens KU-PA as a pan-Minoan grain-associated term and elevates it from PROBABLE to HIGH confidence.

### 2. DI-DI Reduplication as Morphological Feature

The DI-DI reduplication at the beginning of DI-DI-KO-RA-ME-TA₂ is a clear morphological feature. Reduplication is used in many ancient languages for:
- Intensification (Sumerian, Akkadian)
- Plurality (Anatolian languages)
- Iterative/habitual aspect (Indo-European)

This is evidence that Minoan employed productive morphological processes beyond simple suffixation.

### 3. Zakros Mixed Economy

With ZA11a recording GRA+PA, VIN, and OLIV, Zakros is now documented as trading in all three major Cretan agricultural commodities. The site is not purely wine-focused -- earlier characterizations based on ZA15 alone were incomplete.

### 4. Small Wine Quantities as Supplementary

Both VIN entries are ½ unit. Wine appears as a supplement to the primary grain distribution, possibly as a ration addition or ceremonial allocation alongside staple goods.
