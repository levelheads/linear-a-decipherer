## Novel Observations

### 1. VIR-HIDE Compound as Craft Specialization

The compound logogram linking persons (VIR) with hides (HIDE) is unique in the corpus. If it designates leather workers, this provides evidence of craft specialization in the Minoan administrative system -- workers classified by their trade, not just counted as generic labor. This parallels well-documented Near Eastern administrative practices (Ebla, Mari, Ugarit) where workers are categorized by craft.

### 2. Phaistos Administrative Diversity

Phaistos is known primarily for the earliest KU-RO attestation (PH(?)31a, MMIII) and early Luwian morphological features. PH12a adds an industrial/craft dimension to the Phaistos administrative record. The site administered not just commodity distribution but also specialized labor allocation.

### 3. Lames Format

The lames (short thin tablet) is a distinctive Phaistos-associated support type. Its brevity (single compound + quantity) suggests these tablets served as quick-reference records, labels, or tags attached to production lots.
