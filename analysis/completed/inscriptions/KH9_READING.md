**Pattern assessment**: CYP+D with fraction (½) is fully consistent with the Khania grading system (CYP+D = lower grade = fractional quantities). CYP alone with fractions (¼, damaged fraction) is also consistent. No CYP+E appears on this tablet -- only the lower-grade and unqualified copper entries are present, all with fractional quantities.

### Notable Structural Features

1. **A-SI-SU-PO-A as 5-syllable header**: Another long personal name at Khania (cf. A-DA-KI-SI-KA on KH5). The a-prefix and -a ending provide morphological markers for hypothesis testing. Multi-hypothesis support indicates this name does not clearly discriminate between language families.
2. **VIR+*307 with ¹⁄₁₆**: The personnel logogram (VIR) with *307 qualifier is followed by the very small fraction ¹⁄₁₆. This is notable: VIR typically appears with integer quantities (counting people). The ¹⁄₁₆ fraction suggests either partial labor allocation, a time fraction (part-time), or a different measurement system. The ¹⁄₁₆ denomination is also attested on SAMWa1 and KH11.
3. **NI with fraction**: NI (STRONG VIN anchor) with fractional quantity 𐝃𐝁. This is NI at Khania -- confirmed now on KH5, KH9, and KH11 (3 tablets). NI always appears with small quantities at KH.
4. **CYP+D vs CYP distinction**: Both CYP+D (½) and CYP (¼, damaged fraction) appear on this tablet. CYP+D designates the lower copper grade with fractional quantities; CYP alone also appears with fractions here, consistent with the pattern that unqualified CYP can take fractional quantities.
5. **CYP+D ½ vs CYP ¼**: The grade hierarchy is maintained: CYP+D (½) > CYP (¼) in quantity, suggesting CYP+D is a recognized intermediate grade.
6. **VIN without quantity**: VIN appears on line 8 with no visible quantity. This may indicate VIN as a section header, a marker, or the quantity is on a damaged/associated line.
7. **SI+TA2-*304 compound logogram**: A three-element compound logogram. *304 is a commodity logogram (42 occ., 93% word-initial/standalone). SI+TA2-*304 may designate a specific commodity or administrative category. Quantity 1 indicates it is countable.
8. **Damaged entries**: Lines 9-10 contain 𐝫 (damage marker). The CYP fraction on line 9 (𐝃𐝉𐝫) is partially damaged, and line 10 (.3) may be a fragment.
9. **Zero K-R**: No KU-RO, KI-RO, SA-RA₂, or PO-TO-KU-RO. Consistent with Khania zero-K-R pattern.
10. **Mixed personnel + commodity**: VIR+*307 (labor) alongside CYP, VIN, NI, *304. This combination of personnel and commodity accounting on a single tablet is unusual. KH9 may record labor and resources allocated to a single activity or project.

## Novel Observations

### 1. Personnel + Commodity Mixing at Khania

KH9 is the first analyzed Khania tablet to include VIR (personnel) alongside commodity logograms (CYP, VIN). HT tablets frequently mix VIR with commodities (e.g., HT85a VIR allocation, HT88 VIR+KA). The presence of VIR+*307 at Khania proves the KH system tracks labor as well as goods, not just copper transactions.

### 2. VIR+*307 with ¹⁄₁₆ -- Fractional Labor

The ¹⁄₁₆ fraction with a personnel logogram is highly unusual. Personnel are typically counted in integers (whole persons). The fractional allocation may indicate:
- **Partial time allocation**: ¹⁄₁₆ of a labor period or work-gang's effort
- **Wage fraction**: ¹⁄₁₆ of a standard payment or ration
- **Production share**: ¹⁄₁₆ of output attributed to this labor category

This suggests a level of accounting precision in labor management not previously documented at Khania.

### 3. CYP Three-Tier Grading Evidence

KH9 contains CYP+D (½) and CYP (¼) but no CYP+E. Combined with other KH tablets, this supports a three-tier grading system:
- **CYP+E**: Highest grade (integer quantities) -- KH5, KH7a, KH11, KH22
- **CYP+D**: Intermediate grade (larger fractions) -- KH6, KH7a, KH9
- **CYP**: Unqualified/lowest (smaller fractions) -- KH9, KH11, KH29

KH9's CYP+D ½ > CYP ¼ on the same tablet provides the clearest internal evidence for this quantity hierarchy.

### 4. NI at Khania -- Third Confirmation

NI (STRONG VIN anchor, 77 occ., 8 sites) now appears on 3 Khania tablets (KH5, KH9, KH11). All three have wine-related logograms on the same tablet. NI at Khania functions consistently with its pan-Minoan role as a wine-associated term.

### 5. SI+TA2-*304 Compound Logogram

This three-element compound is notable. *304 is a known commodity logogram (42 occ., 5 sites). The SI+TA2 prefix may qualify the commodity type. Compounds like *304+PA (3 occ., multi-site) and *304+PA-CYP+D (copper-graded warehouse commodity) are attested elsewhere. SI+TA2-*304 with quantity 1 suggests a distinct, countable commodity or unit.
