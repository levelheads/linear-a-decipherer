## Novel Observations

### 1. Single-Syllable Administrative Vocabulary at Khania

KH50 continues a pattern seen across the Khania corpus: single-syllable words (TA, JE, QE; compare ZA, SU, SI on KH22). HT tablets tend to use multi-syllable words for recipients and headers. The Khania preference for monosyllabic entries may indicate:
- Abbreviations of longer names (scribal shorthand)
- A different naming convention
- A more condensed administrative notation style

### 2. Small-Scale Distribution

The total of 5 units across 3 recipients is one of the smallest distributions in the analyzed corpus. Most HT tablets record dozens to hundreds of units. This small scale may reflect:
- Precious commodity (metals, spices)
- Daily allocation (vs. summary tablet)
- Sub-section of a larger record
