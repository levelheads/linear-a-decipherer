### Descending Quantity Pattern

The core distribution (lines 3-11) follows a strictly descending sequence:

```
6 → 5 → 5 → 5 → 5 → 4 → 3 → 2 → 1 → ½
```

This is a ranked allocation -- each subsequent recipient receives less than or equal to the prior. The final two entries (TA 2, DU 1) break the pattern slightly, suggesting they may be addenda or belong to a different section.

### Notable Structural Features

1. **Strict descent**: The 6, 5, 5, 5, 5, 4, 3, 2, 1, ½ pattern is one of the clearest descending sequences in the corpus. This parallels HT85a (VIR distribution: 24, 12, 12, 5, 5, 4, 4) -- administrative isomorphism across sites.
2. **13 entries**: One of the longest Zakros distribution lists. ZA4a has 10 entries; ZA10b has 13.
3. **No commodity logogram on most entries**: Only DU-RE-ZA-SE has a logogram (*131B). All other entries are NAME + QUANTITY without commodity specification. The commodity is implied (consistent across all entries).
4. **No KU-RO**: Despite being a list that HT would typically total, no KU-RO appears. This may reflect ZA administrative practice (ZA does not always total) or the commodity may be implied and not require totaling.
5. **MA-ZA and Zakros**: MA-ZA could relate to the site name Zakros. If MA = "from" (directional prefix), MA-ZA = "from Zakros" -- a toponymic reference within a Zakros tablet. This is speculative but phonologically suggestive.
6. **Multiple Luwian suffixes**: -SE (DU-RE-ZA-SE), -TE (MA-KI-DE-TE, KA-KU-NE-TE), -TA (A-MI-TA) all have Luwian morphological support. This is the highest concentration of Luwian-supported names on any Zakros tablet.
7. **RA₂ variant sign**: RA₂-RO-RE uses the same special R-sign found in SA-RA₂ (allocation term). Here it is initial position in a personal name.
8. **WA-*362**: Contains undeciphered *362, limiting analysis. WA- initial may relate to Luwian WA- (quotative or relative particle).
9. **Single-syllable entries**: TA (2) and DU (1) at the end are unusually short -- single-syllable names or abbreviations. They may be scribal shorthand for longer names.

## Novel Observations

### 1. Administrative Isomorphism: HT85a at Zakros

ZA10b's descending ranked allocation is the clearest structural parallel to HT85a outside of Hagia Triada. Both tablets use a NAME + QUANTITY format with strictly decreasing allocations. This proves the ranked distribution template was not an HT scribal idiosyncrasy but a standardized Minoan administrative format used across sites. The isomorphism extends to:
- Both lack explicit headers (HT85a has A-DU as contributor; ZA10b has no clear header)
- Both record 7-13 recipients
- Both show a steep top-to-bottom ratio (HT85a: 24/4 = 6x; ZA10b: 6/0.5 = 12x)

### 2. Luwian Onomastic Cluster at Zakros

Four of 13 entries on ZA10b have Luwian-supported morphology. This is the highest concentration of Luwian-supported personal names on any single Zakros tablet. It suggests that the population recorded in Zakros administrative documents included individuals with Anatolian-influenced names, consistent with the project's finding that Luwian morphological influence is pan-Minoan (not site-specific).

### 3. MA-ZA and the Zakros Toponym Question

MA-ZA appears as a recipient with 5 units. If MA functions as a directional prefix ("from"), MA-ZA could mean "from Zakros" -- a reference to the site itself within its own administrative records. This would be analogous to the attested PA-I-TO (Phaistos) appearing on Phaistos tablets. However, MA-ZA could equally be a personal name. The hypothesis is SPECULATIVE but worth tracking.

### 4. RA₂ in a Personal Name

RA₂-RO-RE uses the RA₂ variant sign (AB 76), the same sign found in SA-RA₂ (the allocation term). In SA-RA₂, the RA₂ sign carries a specific phonetic value distinct from standard RA (AB 60). Its use here in a personal name indicates RA₂ is a regular syllabogram available for personal names, not a sign restricted to administrative terminology.

### 5. The Fractional Recipient

KA-KU-NE-TE receives ½ unit -- the smallest allocation on the tablet. In the HT85a parallel, the smallest allocation is 4 VIR. The ½ here may indicate a different commodity type (where smaller units are meaningful) or that KA-KU-NE-TE holds a very minor position in the administrative hierarchy.
