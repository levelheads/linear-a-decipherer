**No KU-RO total**: Like HT 92, this tablet lacks a totaling line. However, unlike HT 92 (minimal 2-entry list), HT 86a has 6 entries across 2 sections -- a size where KU-RO is commonly present on other tablets. The absence may reflect the two-section structure (no mechanism for combining different grain grades), or it may indicate the tablet is a sub-account within a larger record.

### Notable Structural Features

1. **Dual grain specifiers**: GRA+K+L (Section 1) vs. GRA+B (Section 2). This is analogous to the OLE variant system (OLE+U, OLE+KI, OLE+MI, etc.) documented in KNOWLEDGE.md. The specifiers K+L and B likely denote quality grades, processing states, or grain varieties.
2. **Two supervisor/section-head structure**: A-KA-RU and A-DU each oversee a section. This parallels Near Eastern administrative practice where different officials manage different commodity grades.
3. **Commodity anchor co-occurrence**: Both KU-NI-SU (STRONG anchor, GRA 100%) and DA-ME (STRONG anchor, GRA 100%) appear on the same tablet. This validates their commodity associations and confirms the tablet's grain-distribution function from two independent anchors.
4. **Near-uniform quantities**: Five of six entries are 20; only QA-RA2-WA receives 10 (half-share). This uniformity suggests standardized allocations with a defined exception.
5. **No KU-RO despite 6 entries**: Tablets with comparable entry counts (HT 85a: 7 entries, HT 117a: 10 entries) include KU-RO totals. The absence here is noteworthy.
6. **Dividing line**: Physical horizontal rule between sections. Same scribal convention as HT 117a.
7. **-RU suffix concentration**: Three words end in -RU (A-KA-RU, SA-RU, DI-DE-RU). While -RU is a common ending in the corpus, this concentration on one tablet is notable.

### Commodity Anchor Dual-Function Question

Both **KU-NI-SU** and **DA-ME** are validated STRONG commodity anchors (100% GRA specificity) yet appear in recipient positions on this tablet. Three explanations:

1. **Dual function**: These words serve as both commodity-associated terms (explaining their 100% GRA co-occurrence) AND as personal names/administrative designations. Many Minoan words have attested dual functions (cf. MA-RU as both wool logogram and personal name, DA-RE as both name and transaction marker).

2. **Misclassified position**: The skeleton may misclassify these as "recipients" when they actually serve a different structural role (e.g., commodity descriptors, processing instructions, origin labels).

3. **Specialized GRA administrators**: If KU-NI-SU and DA-ME are officials whose sole role involves grain management, their exclusive GRA co-occurrence would be explained by job function, not word meaning.

**Assessment**: Explanation 1 (dual function) is most parsimonious. The corpus contains other dual-function words, and the positional evidence (NAME + QUANTITY format) strongly supports the recipient reading on this tablet.

The two-section structure distributes different grain grades to different recipient groups. This is analogous to:

- Quality grades (premium vs. standard)
- Grain varieties (wheat vs. barley, or specific cultivars)
- Processing states (milled vs. whole grain)
- Origin labels (from different estates or regions)

The near-uniform allocation of 20 units to 5 of 6 recipients, with QA-RA2-WA receiving exactly half (10), suggests a standardized rationing system. The structure of HT 86a (two sections, each under a different administrator distributing a different grain grade) suggests a bureaucratic division of labor. A-KA-RU manages GRA+K+L distribution; A-DU manages GRA+B distribution.
