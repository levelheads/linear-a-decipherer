## Structural Analysis

### Document Type

**Pure onomastic register -- the ONLY analyzed tablet with exclusively personal names and no quantitative data**

PH6 is unique in the analyzed corpus: it contains nothing but personal names (and possibly a title/kinship term). There are no commodity logograms, no numerals, no totals, no fractions, no administrative markers. This is a name list in its purest form.

### Document Structure

```
[N]  I-NA-WA                     Name 1 (3 syllables; -WA suffix)
     𐄁                           Word divider
[?]  A-RI                        Title/kinship term OR separator (1st occurrence)
---
[N]  I-ZU-RI-NI-TA               Name 2 (5 syllables; -TA suffix)
[?]  A-RI                        Title/kinship term OR separator (2nd occurrence)
---
[N]  I-DA-PA₃-I-SA-RI            Name 3 (6 syllables; -RI ending)
```

### Notable Structural Features

1. **Zero commodities**: No GRA, VIN, OLE, CYP, TELA, VIR, or any other logogram. This is a pure name register.
2. **Zero numerals**: No quantities of any kind. This distinguishes PH6 from every other analyzed tablet.
3. **A-RI repeated**: A-RI appears twice, always following a name. It functions as either:
   - A title or kinship term (e.g., "son of," "of the household of")
   - A category marker or separator
   - A common element of a patronymic formula (Name + A-RI)
4. **I- prefix dominance**: All three names begin with I- (I-NA-WA, I-ZU-RI-NI-TA, I-DA-PA₃-I-SA-RI). This is statistically unlikely to be coincidental and suggests:
   - Patronymic system (all descendants of an I-stem ancestor)
   - Theophoric prefix (divine name element)
   - Ethnic/regional identifier
5. **Name length progression**: 3, 5, 6 syllables -- increasing complexity. The longest name (I-DA-PA₃-I-SA-RI) is among the longest personal names in the corpus.
6. **PA₃ (pa3)**: This sign has NO Linear B equivalent. It is unique to Linear A and represents a phoneme that was lost when Greeks adapted the script. Its presence in the longest name suggests a distinctively Minoan phonological element.
7. **-RI echo**: The ending -RI appears in A-RI (the repeated element) and in I-DA-PA₃-I-SA-**RI** (the final name). This may indicate a morphological connection between the title/kinship term and the third name.
8. **Word divider 𐄁**: Appears only after I-NA-WA (line 1), not between other entries. This may mark I-NA-WA as a header or introductory element distinct from the name+A-RI pairs that follow.

## Novel Observations

### 1. The Only Pure Onomastic Document Analyzed

PH6 is the first analyzed tablet that contains exclusively personal names with no quantitative data whatsoever. Every other reading in the corpus (41 tablets prior) includes at least some combination of logograms, numerals, or administrative markers. PH6 proves that Linear A was used for purely onomastic purposes -- recording names outside of commodity distribution contexts.

### 2. I-Prefix Onomastic Cohort

The three names on PH6 all begin with I-. This systematic I- prefix pattern has no parallel in the analyzed corpus where names are typically diverse in their initial syllables. The I- cohort may represent:
- A patronymic system where family members share an ancestral prefix
- A theophoric naming convention (I- = divine name element)
- An institutional/ethnic identifier (members of a specific clan or profession)

This is significant for understanding Minoan naming conventions and social organization.

### 3. PA₃ as Window into Minoan Phonology

PA₃ (pa3) is one of the most important signs for understanding Minoan phonology because it has no Linear B equivalent. Its presence in a personal name (I-DA-PA₃-I-SA-RI) demonstrates that distinctively Minoan phonemes were embedded in everyday onomastic practice, not restricted to specialized vocabulary. When Greeks adapted Linear A to create Linear B, names containing PA₃ would have been phonologically simplified -- evidence for the "123 dropped signs" phenomenon at the level of personal identity.

### 4. Luwian Onomastic Dominance at Earliest Phaistos

Both testable names on PH6 are Luwian SUPPORTED. Since PH6 may date to MMIII or even earlier (Phaistos spans MMII-LMIB), this strengthens the chronological wedge finding that Luwian morphological features represent the OLDEST contact layer in Linear A. The -WA suffix on I-NA-WA is specifically cited as evidence for early Luwian presence (MMII).

### 5. A-RI as Potential Kinship Term

The repeated A-RI after personal names parallels structures found in Near Eastern administrative texts where kinship or title terms follow names. If A-RI is a kinship term (e.g., "child of," "belonging to"), PH6 may record a lineage or household register. This would be a new document type not previously identified in the corpus.
