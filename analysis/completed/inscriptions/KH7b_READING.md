## Novel Observations

### 1. CYP+K: Third Copper Grade at Khania

CYP+K extends the Khania copper grading system from two grades (CYP+D, CYP+E) to at least three. The K qualifier creates a new copper category. With CYP+K appearing with an integer (2), the grading pattern may be:
- CYP+D = lower grade (fractions)
- CYP+E = higher grade (integers)
- CYP+K = another grade or alloy type (integers)
- CYP = unqualified (mixed, usually fractional)

This is potentially the most elaborate commodity grading system in the Aegean Bronze Age administrative record.

### 2. Luwian Onomastic Cluster at Khania

Both named recipients on KH7b show Luwian morphological support: U-TA-I-SE (3.5) with its -SE suffix and PA-NA-TU (2.0) with its -TU suffix. Combined with KH7a's Luwian-leaning names, the KH7 recto/verso pair presents a coherent picture of Luwian-type personal names in the Khania copper trade administration.

### 3. Equal Fractional Rations

The two named recipients each receive exactly CYP+D 1/2. This equal-portion pattern is unusual -- most HT distribution tablets show variable quantities per recipient. Equal allocations may indicate:
- Standardized copper rations for workers or craftspeople
- A fixed portion system at Khania
- Both recipients holding the same rank or role

### 4. SI with High Integer

SI 14 is a notably high quantity by Khania standards. On KH22, SI receives 4 (CYP+E). Here SI receives 14 with no explicit commodity. If SI is a category rather than a personal name, its quantity may represent a different commodity entirely from the copper entries above.
