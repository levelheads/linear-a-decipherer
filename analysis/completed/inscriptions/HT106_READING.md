## Structural Analysis

### Document Type

**Two-section CYP (copper) account**

HT 106 records copper quantities in two sections separated by a ruled dividing line. MI-NU-TE heads Section 1; DA heads Section 2.

### Document Structure

```
=== Section 1: MI-NU-TE ===
[H]   MI-NU-TE                      Header: source/contributor
[C]   CYP                    6      Commodity: 6 copper units
[R]   SI                     5.5    Entry 1: 5.5 copper units
[R]   SI                     1      Entry 2: 1 copper unit
                                    (Section 1 sum = 12.5)
=== Divider ===
[—]                                  Explicit section boundary

=== Section 2: DA ===
[H]   DA  𐄁                          Header: source/category
[#]   8                              Orphan quantity (8 copper units)
[?]   SI  3                          Entry: 3 copper units
[?]   𐝫                              Damaged (tablet broken)
```

### Notable Structural Features

1. **CYP at Hagia Triada**: Copper (CYP) is predominantly a Khania commodity. Its appearance at HT is unusual. Most HT tablets deal with GRA, VIN, VIR, or OLE. HT 106 is one of the few HT tablets with CYP.

2. **SI as recurring element**: SI appears 3 times (Lines 2, 3, 7) with different quantities. In the corpus, SI has 112 occurrences -- it is extremely common. On this tablet, SI may function as:
   - A sub-category or grade of copper (like CYP+D vs CYP+E at Khania)
   - A recipient/location name (abbreviated)
   - A measurement unit

3. **Fractions present**: ¹⁄₂ on Line 2 indicates fractional copper units. This contrasts with the VIR tablets (always integers) and parallels KH copper tablets which also use fractions.

4. **MI-NU-TE as header**: On the GRA cohort tablets, MI-NU-TE is a grain RECIPIENT. Here it is a copper CONTRIBUTOR/SOURCE. This role reversal demonstrates administrative flexibility -- an entity that receives grain from one source can contribute copper to another.

5. **Explicit section divider**: The ruled line (—) on Line 4 is an explicit scribal mark separating two sections. This is rare on HT tablets and parallels the divider on HT 86a between Section 1 and Section 2.

3. **CYP at HT indicates trade network**: HT is not a copper production site (unlike KH). CYP on HT 106 may represent copper received through trade, tribute, or redistribution.

## Novel Observations

### 1. MI-NU-TE as Cross-Commodity Administrator

MI-NU-TE's role reversal (GRA recipient -> CYP header) is the most dramatic example of administrative role-switching in the corpus. If MI-NU-TE is a personal name, this indicates the same individual manages both grain receipts and copper distributions. If MI-NU-TE is an institutional name (workshop, estate), the dual commodity involvement is expected.

### 2. CYP at Hagia Triada

CYP is predominantly a Khania commodity (CYP+D fractions, CYP+E integers). Its appearance on an HT tablet confirms that copper circulated within the HT administrative system, not just at the port city of Khania. HT 106 may document copper received from Khania or another source.

### 3. SI's Multi-Context Function

SI's 112 occurrences make it one of the most common monosyllables in the corpus. On HT 106, its 3 appearances with copper quantities may represent a measurement qualifier, a copper sub-type, or an abbreviated institutional name. The same ambiguity applies to DA (9 occurrences).
