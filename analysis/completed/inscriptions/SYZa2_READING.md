## Structural Analysis

### Document Type

**UNIQUE HYBRID: Libation formula with embedded commodity logograms**

SYZa2 is unlike any other analyzed inscription. It combines standard libation formula elements (A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA, U-NA-KA-NA-SI) with commodity logograms (OLIV, OLE) that are normally exclusive to administrative texts. The commodity logograms are ligatured directly to the formula words, creating hybrid notations: JA-SU-MA-TU-OLIV and U-NA-KA-NA-SI-OLE.

This is the ONLY known inscription that bridges the religious and administrative registers.

### Document Structure

```
[Pos 1]  A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA       Main verb (libation formula)
         𐄁                        Word divider
[Hybrid] JA-SU-MA-TU-OLIV        Name/term + OLIV ligature (olive offering?)
[#]      1                        Quantity: 1 unit of OLIV
[Hybrid] U-NA-KA-NA-SI-OLE       Ritual verb + OLE ligature (oil offering?)
[?]      A-JA                    Modifier / conjunction / closing
```

### Notable Structural Features

1. **Commodity-formula hybrid**: OLIV and OLE logograms ligatured to formula words. This is unique in the corpus. No other libation inscription includes commodity logograms.
2. **JA-SA-SA-RA-ME absent**: The divine name (position 4) is missing. Instead, commodity offerings are specified. This may indicate a different ritual context -- an offering of physical goods (olives + oil) rather than a purely verbal invocation.
3. **Form A main verb**: A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA with -WA-JA suffix (not -WA-E). Consistent with Form A.
4. **U-NA-KA-NA-SI-OLE**: The ritual verb is ligatured with OLE, suggesting the verb's action involves olive oil specifically.
5. **JA-SU-MA-TU-OLIV**: A 4-syllable word ligatured with OLIV. Could be: dedicant's name + offering type, or a qualified offering description.
6. **A-JA closing**: hypothesis_tester best=luwian PROBABLE. Luwian conjunction *aya* "and" or demonstrative. As a closing element, it may serve as a formula terminator.
7. **Quantity 1**: Only 1 unit of OLIV recorded. This is a small, specific offering, not a large distribution.

## What We Hypothesize

1. **Commodity-offering specification**: The ligatures (JA-SU-MA-TU-OLIV, U-NA-KA-NA-SI-OLE) specify the physical offerings accompanying the verbal invocation. Instead of invoking a divine name, this formula records what was actually offered. PROBABLE.
2. **A-JA as Luwian conjunction**: A-JA = *aya* "and" or demonstrative. This could close the formula: "[s/he] invokes... [offering olives]... and offers oil." PROBABLE.
3. **JA-SU-MA-TU as dedicant**: A 4-syllable personal name of the person making the offering. POSSIBLE.
4. **Alternative: JA-SU-MA-TU as offering descriptor**: The word could describe the type of olive offering rather than naming the dedicant. POSSIBLE.
5. **Hybrid function**: SYZa2 bridges religious and economic registers, recording both the ritual act and the commodity given. This may indicate that at Kato Symi, libation formulas served a dual purpose: religious invocation AND economic accounting of temple offerings. POSSIBLE.

## Novel Observations

### 1. First Religious-Administrative Hybrid

SYZa2 is the ONLY known inscription that combines libation formula elements with commodity logograms. This bridges the two registers that are otherwise strictly separate:
- Administrative register: commodity logograms, numerals, K-R vocabulary, personal names
- Religious register: libation formula, divine names, ritual verbs, no numerals

SYZa2 proves that the registers could overlap in specific ritual-economic contexts (temple offerings).

### 2. Absence of JA-SA-SA-RA-ME

Every other libation inscription includes JA-SA-SA-RA-ME (or SA-SA-RA-ME) in position 4. SYZa2 replaces the divine name with commodity offerings. Two interpretations:
- **Replacement**: The commodity specification replaces the divine invocation (the deity is known from context at Kato Symi)
- **Different formula type**: SYZa2 is an offering receipt, not an invocation. The verb A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA describes the act; the commodities describe what was given

### 3. Olive Economy at Kato Symi

Both commodities on SYZa2 are olive-based: OLIV (olives, raw fruit) and OLE (olive oil, processed product). This olive focus at a religious site may indicate:
- Olives and olive oil as standard temple offerings
- Kato Symi's location in an olive-growing region
- Olive products as ritually significant commodities

### 4. A-JA as Formula Closing

A-JA in final position is unique among libation formulas (which typically end with SI-RU-TE + I-PI-NA-MA). As a Luwian conjunction (*aya* "and"), it may connect the two offerings: "...olives AND oil." Or it may serve as a formulaic closing marker distinct from the standard prepositional phrase.

### 5. Dual Kato Symi Libation Traditions

SY Za 3-4 uses the standard libation formula (A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA + JA-SA-SA-RA-ME). SYZa2 uses a hybrid formula without JA-SA-SA-RA-ME but with commodity logograms. Two different ritual inscription types coexist at the same site, parallel to the Form A/B coexistence at Palaikastro.
