### Notable Structural Features

1. **DI-RA-DI-NA reduplication**: The sequence DI-RA is repeated: DI-RA-DI-NA. Reduplication is a significant morphological feature that occurs in several language families (intensification, plurality, iterative aspect). This is the most transparent reduplicated form in the analyzed Phaistos corpus.
2. ***316 appears twice**: This Phaistos-specific logogram occurs in both line 1 (with DI-RA-DI-NA) and line 2 (with JA and quantity 1). A logogram appearing twice on a short tablet suggests it is the primary commodity or category being recorded.
3. **CYP at Phaistos**: Copper (CYP) is primarily associated with Khania in the analyzed corpus (CYP+D fractions, CYP+E integers at KH). Its presence at Phaistos demonstrates that copper administration was not exclusively a Khania function.
4. **Fractional CYP (¹⁄₆)**: The fractional copper quantity parallels Khania practice. At KH, unqualified CYP takes fractions (CYP+E takes integers). PH1a's CYP ≈ ¹⁄₆ follows the same pattern: unqualified CYP with a fraction.
5. **≈ sign anomaly**: The ≈ sign is a STRONG VIN anchor (100% VIN specificity, 10 occ., 6 sites per KNOWLEDGE.md). Its appearance here with CYP (not VIN) is an **anomaly**. Possible explanations:
   - ≈ has a broader function (approximation marker) beyond VIN contexts
   - The sign here is visually similar but functionally different from the VIN ≈
   - This is a genuine exception to the 100% VIN specificity
6. **JA standalone**: JA appears as an independent entry (line 2), not as a suffix on a word. As a standalone morpheme, JA may function as a genitive marker, category qualifier, or abbreviated reference. The -JA suffix is the most productive Luwian morphological feature in the corpus (65.9% word-final).
7. **Short tablet**: Only 4 lines (with line 4 damaged). This is a minimal record -- possibly a label, receipt, or single-transaction document.

## Novel Observations

### 1. CYP at Phaistos Expands Copper Administration Geography

PH1a proves copper was administered at Phaistos, expanding the known CYP geography beyond Khania (primary) and Hagia Triada (HT106). The fractional quantity (≈ ¹⁄₆) follows the KH pattern where unqualified CYP takes fractions, suggesting a shared understanding of copper measurement conventions across sites -- even where the full CYP grading system (CYP+D, CYP+E) is not employed.

### 2. DI-RA-DI-NA: Transparent Two-Syllable Reduplication

The DI-RA-DI-NA reduplication (DI-RA repeated twice, then -NA suffix) is the most transparent reduplicative morphological operation in the analyzed Phaistos corpus. While gemination (SA-SA in JA-SA-SA-RA-ME) doubles a single syllable, DI-RA-DI repeats a disyllabic unit. This is typologically significant:
- In Anatolian languages, reduplication indicates iterative or intensive meaning
- In Semitic, reduplication can indicate pluralization
- The pattern alone does not discriminate hypotheses, but its presence demonstrates that Linear A morphology includes productive reduplication

### 3. ≈ Sign Beyond VIN Context

The ≈ sign, previously a 100% VIN-specific anchor (10 occ., 6 sites), appears here with CYP. If confirmed, this is the first breach of the ≈-VIN exclusivity. The sign may have a broader function as an approximation marker ("approximately") that happens to co-occur primarily with wine measurements. This finding should prompt a corpus-wide review of ≈ contexts to determine whether other exceptions exist in uninvestigated tablets.

### 4. *316 as Phaistos Administrative Logogram

*316 appearing twice on PH1a (with fraction 𐝉 and with integer 1) parallels how CYP appears on Khania tablets -- a local commodity logogram used in multiple entries on a single tablet. *316 may represent a Phaistos-specific commodity or administrative category (textile? ceramic? agricultural product?). Its co-occurrence with CYP suggests a mixed-commodity record.

### 5. JA as Independent Morpheme

JA appearing standalone (not suffixed) on PH1a is significant. If -JA is the Luwian adjectival suffix -iya, its standalone use may indicate:
- An abbreviated reference to a previously specified JA-qualified term
- A morpheme functioning independently as a category marker
- A different morpheme from the suffix -JA (homophone with different function)

This supports the possibility that -JA has a broader grammatical role than just adjectival suffixation.
