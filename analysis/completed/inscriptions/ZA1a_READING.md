## Structural Analysis

### Document Type

**A commodity record at Zakros containing the LARGEST NI quantity in the entire corpus (42½)**

ZA1a is a short inscription (4 lines, final line damaged) that records an exceptionally large quantity of NI (42½ units). NI is the STRONG VIN (wine) anchor with 100% specificity across 77 occurrences at 8 sites. If NI = wine, then 42½ units represents a major wine allocation -- the largest single NI entry known.

### Notable Structural Features

1. **NI 42½ = record quantity**: This is the LARGEST NI quantity in the entire corpus. Typical NI quantities are 1-10 units. A quantity of 42½ is 4-40x larger than the norm, indicating institutional-scale allocation or a bulk transaction.
2. ***516 as Zakros logogram**: *516 is attested at Zakros. Its appearance on ZA11a as well (DI-DI-KO-RA-ME-TA₂ + *516) confirms it is a Zakros-specific sign, possibly a local commodity or institutional marker.
3. **KI-RE-ZA + NI**: A personal or institutional name followed by the STRONG VIN anchor. The KI- prefix element appears in KI-RO (deficit), KI-SA-NE, and other terms, but its function here is likely different -- part of a proper name rather than an administrative prefix.
4. **ZA as single syllable**: The standalone "ZA" with quantity 5 is unusual. Possible interpretations:
   - Abbreviation for Zakros (the site itself)
   - Shortened form of a longer name
   - A commodity sub-type or qualifier
5. **Damaged final line**: The 𐝫 indicates damage -- the inscription may have continued with additional entries or a total marker.
6. **Word dividers**: Two 𐄁 dividers provide clear structural separation, indicating careful scribal formatting.

## Novel Observations

### 1. Record NI Quantity: 42½ Units

NI 42½ is the single largest NI allocation in the entire Linear A corpus. If NI = wine (100% VIN specificity), this represents an enormous wine allocation -- far exceeding typical distributions (1-10 units). For context:
- HT 13 (largest VIN distribution) has KU-RO 130.5 VIN spread across 6+ recipients
- ZA 15b has KU-RO 78 VIN
- ZA1a allocates 42½ units to a SINGLE entry (KI-RE-ZA)

This quantity suggests ZA1a records a bulk institutional transaction, possibly an annual supply, a major feast allocation, or a shipment to an estate.

### 2. NI at Zakros: Pan-Minoan Confirmation

NI is now confirmed at Zakros, adding to the already extensive site distribution (HT, KH, PH, and 4+ others). This is consistent with wine being a pan-Minoan administered commodity. Zakros's documented wine focus (ZA15, ZA4a) makes the NI appearance here expected but important for verification.

### 3. *516 as Zakros Administrative Marker

*516 appears on two Zakros tablets (ZA1a and ZA11a) and nowhere else in the corpus. On ZA11a, it accompanies DI-DI-KO-RA-ME-TA₂ (a 6-syllable header) and precedes a mixed GRA+PA/VIN/OLIV distribution. On ZA1a, it stands alone in line 1 before a word divider. This pattern suggests *516 is a Zakros-specific institutional or administrative marker -- possibly a local equivalent of administrative qualifiers used at other sites.

### 4. Absence of Explicit VIN Logogram

NI is a STRONG VIN anchor (100% specificity), but the VIN logogram itself does not appear on ZA1a. This is significant: the scribe used NI as the commodity identifier without the pictographic VIN sign. This supports the interpretation that NI is not merely wine-associated but may BE the wine term in syllabographic notation -- or that NI is a qualifier/type that makes VIN redundant.
