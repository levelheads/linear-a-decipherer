## Novel Observations

### 1. Most Commodity-Diverse Khania Tablet

KH8 records at least 7 distinct commodity types: CYP+D, CYP, GRA, NI (wine), VIR+[?], *304, *312. This exceeds KH11 (CYP, CYP+E, VIN, *306, *301 = 5 types) and is the most diverse KH tablet analyzed. The diversity suggests Khania's administration tracked a wide range of goods, not just the copper for which the site is known.

### 2. NI as Standalone Wine Marker at Khania

NI appears twice on KH8 without an accompanying VIN logogram. This is consistent with KNOWLEDGE.md's NI anchor (100% VIN specificity, STRONG). At Khania, NI may serve as the sole wine marker without the VIN logogram, which is more common at HT. This suggests NI is the primary wine-designation term, with VIN as a secondary (or redundant) logographic marker.

### 3. Fractional Micro-Economy

Every commodity entry on KH8 is a fraction (1/2 or 1/4) or minimal integer (1 or 2). This contrasts with the large-scale distributions at HT (e.g., HT102 GRA 1060; HT13 VIN 130.5). The micro-allocation pattern at Khania may reflect:
- Daily rations for a small workforce
- Sampling or quality-control distributions
- Sub-ledger entries that feed into a larger (lost) master tablet

### 4. GRA Expanding the Khania Commodity Set

GRA has been HT-dominant in the analyzed corpus. Its presence at KH8 (alongside CYP+D, CYP, NI, VIR, *304, *312) proves that Khania's economy included grain administration. The documented KH commodity set now includes: CYP, CYP+D, CYP+E, CYP+K, VIN, NI, GRA, VIR, *304, *306, *312.
