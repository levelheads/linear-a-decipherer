## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Knossos Ivory Scepter represents the most significant Linear A discovery in decades. Found in the Ivory Repository of the Anetaki plot Fetish Shrine, it contains:

- **119 signs** - longest Linear A inscription ever discovered
- **Dual register** - ceremonial (ring) + administrative (handle)
- **Two scribes** - different hands with different styles
- **Mixed scripts** - Linear A + Cretan Hieroglyphic signs
- **No numerals on ring** - distinguishes from administrative texts
- **Numerals + fractions on handle** - first Linear A economic document from cult context

## PART 2: INSCRIPTION STRUCTURE

### 2.1 KN Zg 57 (Ivory Ring) - Ceremonial Inscription

**Total Signs**: ~119 (84 completely/partially preserved + 35 traces/probable)

**Four Faces**:
- Face A (top, sloped): Animals + Vases with ligatures
- Face B (bottom, flat): Sign groups + logograms (GRA, FAR, OLIV)
- Face C (exterior): Sign groups + textile logograms
- Face D (interior): Hide logograms (severely damaged)

**Critical Feature**: **NO NUMERALS** - aligns with stone libation vessels, not administrative texts

**Writing Direction**: Left-to-right (confirmed by orientation of AB 60, AB 01, AB 81, etc.)

**Layout**: Metope system (vertical lines creating framed spaces for each sign/image)

### 2.2 KN Zg 58 (Ivory Handle) - Administrative Inscription

**Four Faces** (α, β, γ, δ):
- Face α: Vases with ligatures + numeral "10" + Hieroglyphic signs *180, *181
- Face β: 4-sign sequence + hide sign
- Face γ: Boar and pig signs (no numerals)
- Face δ: Vases with ligatures + units + **sequence of 6 fractions**

**Critical Feature**: **NUMERALS AND FRACTIONS PRESENT** - first Linear A economic document from cult context

## PART 3: RING FACE-BY-FACE ANALYSIS

### 3.1 Face A (Top Face)

**Left Half** (counter-clockwise from 6 o'clock):
- **12 quadruped animals** in metopes
- All animals facing left
- Likely goat-type animals (detailed iconography forthcoming)
- **INTERPRETATION**: Sacrificial animals for ritual feast

**Right Half** (clockwise from 12 o'clock):
- **16+ signs preserved** (estimated 18 originally)
- **10+ vases** in iconographic style
- Vase types: 6 amphorae, tripod, rhyton (AB 664)

**Vases with Syllabic Ligatures**:
Signs emerge upward from vessel mouths, indicating contents or type:

| Vessel | Ligature | AB# | Possible Meaning |
|--------|----------|-----|------------------|
| Amphora | +PA | AB 03 | pa-? (content) |
| Amphora | +RU | AB 26 | ru-? (content) |
| Amphora | +RA | AB 60 | ra-? (content) |
| Amphora | +I | AB 28 | i-? (content) |
| Amphora | +NE | AB 24 | ne-? (content) |
| Amphora | +SE | AB 09 | se-? (content) |
| Tripod | (none) | — | Shape self-identifying |
| Rhyton | (none) | AB 664 | Shape self-identifying |

**Key Observation**: Tripod and rhyton lack ligatures because their shapes are unmistakable.

### 3.2 Face B (Bottom Face)

**Content**: Sign groups + single syllabograms + logograms

**Preserved Sign Groups**: 6 well-preserved groups

**Logograms Present**:
| Logogram | Linear B Value | Meaning |
|----------|----------------|---------|
| GRA | grain | Cereal/grain |
| FAR | *far* | Flour/barley |
| OLIV | olives | Olives |

**No numerals** - quantities not specified

**Direction**: Left-to-right (per AB 81, AB 40, AB 60 orientation)

### 3.3 Face C (Exterior Face)

**Condition**: ~40% surface damage (no traces in damaged areas)

**Content**:
- At least 9 sign groups
- 5 textile logograms (TELA variants)
- Textiles differentiated by number of fringes

**Textile Ligature**: One textile sign ligatured with AB 77 (KA) inside

**INTERPRETATION**: Textile offerings for religious ceremony

### 3.4 Face D (Interior Face)

**Condition**: Extremely damaged

**Content**:
- ~9 hide ideograms (AB 180)
- Each in separate metope
- None entirely preserved

**Ligature Evidence**: One hide + possible AB 77 (KA), AB 78 (QE), or AB 70 (KO)

**Parallel**: Form matches Phaistos Linear A tablets

## PART 4: HANDLE FACE-BY-FACE ANALYSIS

### 4.1 Face α

**Content**:
- 2 probable vases with phonetic ligatures
- Numeral sign for 10
- Probable NEW sign (not in Linear A repertory)

**Significance**: Confirms *180 and *181 were used in Linear A (previously only Hieroglyphic)

### 4.2 Face β

**Content**:
- Right end: 4-sign sequence (first sign damaged)
- Left half: Hide sign (Phaistos-type form)

### 4.3 Face γ

**Content**:
- Boar sign
- Probable pig sign
- **No numerals**

### 4.4 Face δ

**Content**:
- 2 vases with ligatures
- Unit numerals
- **SEQUENCE OF 6 FRACTION SIGNS**

**MAJOR DISCOVERY**: This fraction sequence may reveal relative values of Linear A fractions - potentially revising all previous interpretations of the fractional system.

## PART 5: PALEOGRAPHIC ANALYSIS

### 5.1 Two Distinct Hands

**Evidence for Different Scribes**:

| Feature | Ring (KN Zg 57) | Handle (KN Zg 58) |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Style** | Calligraphic, iconographic | Linearized, standard |
| **AB 08 (A)** | Same form throughout, calligraphic | Linearized form |
| **AB 77 (KA)** | Consistent internal strokes | Different stroke orientation |
| **AB 08 + AB 77 ligature** | AB 08 ABOVE AB 77 | AB 08 BELOW AB 77 |
| **Overall ductus** | Very calligraphic | More cursive |

**Conclusion**: Two different hands (scribes) produced these inscriptions.

### 5.2 Stylistic Comparisons

**Ring Style Parallels**:
- Stone libation table from Iouktas (IO Za 2) - metope arrangement
- Painted cup inscriptions (KN Zc 6, KN Zc 7)
- Ring of Mavro Spelio (KN Zf 13)
- Silver pin from Knossos (KN Zf 31) - signs AB 60, AB 28, AB 24, AB 09
- Phaistos Disk - stamped signs in metopes
- Cretan Hieroglyphic seals - accuracy and technique

**Unique Feature**: First Linear A inscription in predominantly **calligraphic style**. Previous Linear A was either cursive (tablets) or merely accurate (libation tables). The Ring shows "lifelike, artistic, close to three-dimensional" signs.

## PART 8: MULTI-HYPOTHESIS TESTING

### 8.1 Luwian Hypothesis

**Evidence FOR**:
- Particles: Need to check for a-, -wa-, -ja patterns in sign groups
- Verbal endings: -ti/-nti would support Luwian
- Religious context could show Anatolian divine names

**Evidence AGAINST**:
- Insufficient sign group data for thorough testing
- No clear divine names identified yet

**Verdict**: INSUFFICIENT DATA (awaiting full transliteration)

### 8.2 Semitic Hypothesis

**Evidence FOR**:
- Triconsonantal structure would support (need consonant extraction)
- Administrative loans possible on handle
- Religious vocabulary could include Northwest Semitic elements

**Evidence AGAINST**:
- Morphological patterns unclear
- No clear Semitic roots identified yet

**Verdict**: INSUFFICIENT DATA (awaiting full transliteration)

### 8.3 Pre-Greek Hypothesis

**Evidence FOR**:
- Unique vocabulary (few parallels with other Linear A texts)
- Religious context fits substrate divine names
- -ss-, -nth- patterns would support (cannot test in CV syllabary)

**Evidence AGAINST**:
- Cannot directly test phonological markers

**Verdict**: COMPATIBLE but unverified

### 8.4 Proto-Greek Hypothesis

**Evidence FOR**:
- Logograms match Linear B (GRA, FAR, OLIV, TELA)
- Metope system parallels Phaistos Disk

**Evidence AGAINST**:
- No Greek divine names identified
- Low /o/ frequency problem remains
- ku-ro absent (Greek would use to-so)

**Verdict**: WEAK (consistent with broader corpus findings)

## PART 9: INTERPRETIVE SYNTHESIS

### 9.1 Ring Function

**Authors' Interpretation** (Kanta et al.):
> "The aim of the creator of Face A was to record, with pure iconography, the animals and then, in their most realistic forms, using the repertory of Linear A logograms, the vases, the textiles and the hides..."

**Proposed Function**: **Ritual feast record or prescriptive mnemonic**

The Ring records "all the elements necessary for a ritual feasting ceremony":
- 12 animals (sacrifices)
- 10+ vessels with contents specified
- Grain, flour, olives (offerings)
- Textiles (dedicatory garments?)
- Hides (processed animal products?)

**Parallels**:
- Linear B ritual texts (ingredients lists)
- Linear A tablet counterparts (see Kanta forthcoming)
- Hittite ritual prescriptions (mnemonic texts)

### 9.2 Handle Function

**Authors' Interpretation**:
> "The first Linear A economic document coming from a cult building"

**Proposed Function**: **Administrative accounting for ritual supplies**

Evidence:
- Numerals present
- Fractions present (6-sign sequence)
- Cretan Hieroglyphic signs (*180, *181) - archaic administrative elements
- Different scribe from Ring

### 9.3 Ring + Handle Relationship

**Scenario 1: Sequential Creation**
- Ring inscribed first (ceremonial dedication)
- Handle added later (administrative addendum)
- Different scribes = different occasions

**Scenario 2: Simultaneous Specialized**
- Ring: Religious scribe (formal training)
- Handle: Administrative scribe (practical training)
- Same occasion, different specialists

**Scenario 3: Dual-Function Object**
- Scepter designed for both sacred and secular authority
- Ring = ritual power
- Handle = bureaucratic record
