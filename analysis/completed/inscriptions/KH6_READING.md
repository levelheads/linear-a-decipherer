6. ***306 and *334**: Both are recurring KH logograms of unknown identity. Their presence on KH6 alongside CYP records extends their attestation.
7. ***21M masculine classifier**: Appears with quantity 1 (line 8). Its appearance on a CYP+D tablet between recipient names suggests a personnel categorization function.
8. **Multi-syllable names**: DI-SI, TE-NU-RE, KI-SA-NE, RI-TA-JE, PI-SA, AU-RE-TE — notably more multi-syllable names than KH22 or KH50 (which used single-syllable entries). This allows meaningful hypothesis testing.

## Novel Observations

### 1. Purest CYP+D Concentration

KH6 is the most CYP+D-concentrated tablet in the analyzed Khania corpus. Five entries of CYP+D ½ demonstrate that the CYP+D = fractional pattern is not occasional but systematic. The consistency (all ½) suggests a standardized allocation unit for lower-grade copper at Khania: one-half unit per recipient.

### 2. Total in Unqualified CYP

The total line (CYP 2 𐝆𐝁) uses unqualified CYP rather than CYP+D. This implies that for summation purposes, the grade qualifier is dropped — the total reports copper regardless of grade. This is analogous to modern accounting where subtypes are summed into a generic category for the bottom line.

### 3. -JE as Morphological E-Grade

The -JE suffix on RI-TA-JE, if related to -JA (Luwian -iya), would parallel the WA-JA / WA-E alternation documented in the libation formula inflectional paradigm (PKZa11 Form B). This suggests vowel alternation is a productive morphological process in Minoan, not limited to religious texts. Finding it on an administrative copper tablet at Khania expands the scope of the vowel alternation paradigm.

### 4. AU-RE-TE: Luwian Morphology at Khania

AU-RE-TE (Luwian SUPPORTED, 2.0) is the strongest linguistic signal on any KH copper tablet. The a-prefix + -te suffix pattern matches Anatolian verbal/nominal derivation. Combined with the domain-layering model (Religious=Luwian, Admin=Semitic), this suggests that even at Khania — which operates a parallel administrative system — Luwian morphological influence penetrates personal naming.

### 5. Khania Totaling Without KU-RO

KH6 provides a total ("CYP 2 𐝆𐝁") without using KU-RO. This confirms that Khania has its own totaling convention. The HT system uses KU-RO (total) + KI-RO (deficit) for accounting; Khania simply states the commodity symbol and sum. This is further evidence for parallel but distinct administrative systems.
