## Structural Analysis

### Document Type

**Large-scale deficit/distribution record from QE-RA₂-U**

HT 1 records massive quantities (hundreds) under QE-RA₂-U's authority, with KI-RO = 197 as an opening deficit or category marker. No commodity logogram appears.

### Document Structure

```
[H]   QE-RA₂-U  𐄁                    Header: institutional source
[D]   KI-RO             197          Deficit/opening balance: 197 units
[R]   ZU-SU              70          Recipient 1: 70 units
[R]   DI-DI-ZA-KE        52          Recipient 2: 52 units
[R]   KU-PA₃-NU         109          Recipient 3: 109 units (LARGEST)
[R]   A-RA-NA-RE         105          Recipient 4: 105 units
```

### Notable Structural Features

1. **QE-RA₂-U as header (role reversal)**: QE-RA₂-U is a GRA recipient on HT 95a (7 units) and HT 95b (10 units) -- the smallest allocation in the GRA cohort. Here, QE-RA₂-U is the HEADER of a massive distribution (336+ units). This is the fourth documented role reversal:
   - MI-NU-TE: GRA recipient -> CYP header (HT 106)
   - KU-NI-SU: GRA recipient -> GRA source (HT 10a)
   - QE-RA₂-U: GRA recipient (smallest!) -> header of largest-scale distribution

2. **Hundred-scale quantities**: 197, 109, 105, 70, 52. These are the largest numbers on any analyzed HT tablet. The scale dwarfs all previous readings (HT 11b KU-RO 180 was previously the largest single number). KI-RO 197 is the largest single entry.

3. **KI-RO as opening entry**: KI-RO appears immediately after the header (Line 2), before any recipient entries. On HT 88, KI-RO appeared between sections. On HT 123+124a, KI-RO appeared per-entry. Here, KI-RO precedes all entries, suggesting it represents either:
   - An opening deficit (what QE-RA₂-U owes before distributions)
   - A carried-forward balance from a previous period
   - A deduction category separate from the four distributions

4. **KU-PA₃-NU receives 109 (largest entry)**: KU-PA₃-NU appears on 7 tablets but always with small quantities (1-6 units). Here, 109 units represents a massive scale-up. This may indicate:
   - KU-PA₃-NU is an institution (not individual) that operates at different scales
   - HT 1 records a different commodity type (one measured in large units)
   - The quantities may represent area, weight, or a unit different from the standard measures

5. **No commodity logogram**: The unspecified commodity could be land area, agricultural output, textile, or any bulk commodity measured in hundreds. The lack of GRA/VIN/OLE/VIR makes identification impossible without additional evidence.

6. **Findspot**: Portico 11 and Room 13 -- a different findspot from the Casa Room 7 and Villa Magazine clusters. This may indicate a different administrative archive.

## Novel Observations

### 1. QE-RA₂-U's Administrative Scale

QE-RA₂-U is the smallest GRA recipient in the cohort (7-10 units) but administers the largest distribution in our readings (336+ units). The grain it receives may be personal provisioning, while it manages a much larger operation involving hundreds of units of an unspecified commodity. This is the most dramatic demonstration of the difference between "receiving rations" and "administering distributions."

### 2. Largest KI-RO in the Corpus

KI-RO = 197 is by far the largest deficit recorded. On HT 88, the implicit KI-RO = 1. On HT 123+124a, KI-RO entries total 6. Here, 197 represents a deficit larger than any single verified KU-RO total except HT 11b (180). This suggests the underlying operation is extremely large-scale.

### 3. KU-PA₃-NU's Dual Scale

KU-PA₃-NU receives 1 unit on HT 88 (VIR), 1 on HT 122a (VIR), and small amounts on other tablets. Here, 109 units -- over 100x larger. This confirms KU-PA₃-NU operates at multiple scales depending on commodity/context and is likely an institution, not an individual.
