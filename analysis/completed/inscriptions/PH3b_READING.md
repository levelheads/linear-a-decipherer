### Notable Features

4. **SI repeated**: SI appears twice with different quantities (¼ and 1¼). SI may be a category marker, quality grade, or abbreviation.

## Novel Observations

### Opisthographic Practice at Phaistos

PH3a (object inventory) and PH3b (fractional commodity record) are different document types on the same tablet. This demonstrates that Minoan scribes reused tablet surfaces for different administrative purposes -- an economic practice reflecting writing material constraints.

### -JA Suffix in Header Position

The -JA suffix at the header of a commodity record is notable. If -JA is adjectival (Luwian -iya), then the header describes a qualified type: "[X]-MI-JA = the MI-type [commodity]." This connects administrative vocabulary to Luwian morphological patterns.
