## What We Know For Certain

1. **Commodity**: GRA (grain). Established by logogram in header.

2. **Distribution document**: 6 named recipients receive grain allocations. Standard structure.

3. **GRA cohort membership**: 5 of 6 recipients appear on HT 86a and HT 95b, forming the strongest cross-tablet name cluster in the corpus.

4. **Dual commodity anchors**: KU-NI-SU (100% GRA, n=5) and DA-ME (100% GRA, n=4) both appear, providing independent confirmation of the grain context.

## What We Hypothesize

1. **DA-DU-MA-TA as institutional source**: Header position supports administrative function. The 4-syllable structure and hapax status limit interpretation. May be an institution name, estate, or administrative category.

2. **SA-RU as senior recipient**: Consistently the largest or tied-largest recipient across the GRA cohort (20 on 95a, 10 on 95b, 20 on 86a). May hold a supervisory position or larger estate.

3. **QE-RA₂-U as junior/subsidiary**: Receives only 7 (below the modal 10). Not present on HT 86a. May be a newer or smaller participant in the grain distribution network.

4. **The GRA cohort represents a stable administrative unit**: The same 5-6 names receiving grain across 3 tablets implies a persistent group -- perhaps an estate, department, or settlement receiving regular grain allocations from Hagia Triada palace.

5. **Different headers = different accounting periods or sources**: DA-DU-MA-TA (95a), A-DU (95b), A-KA-RU/A-DU (86a) all distribute grain to the same group. These may be different time periods, different grain types (note GRA+K+L vs. GRA+B on 86a), or different administrative purposes.

6. **Absence of KU-RO may indicate this is a working document**: Without a total, this may be a draft, partial record, or running distribution log rather than a finalized account.

## Novel Observations

### 1. The GRA Distribution Cohort is the Strongest Cross-Tablet Network

The set {DA-ME, MI-NU-TE, SA-RU, KU-NI-SU, DI-DE-RU} appears together on HT 86a, HT 95a, and HT 95b -- a 3-tablet complete match. Adding QE-RA₂-U gives HT 95a/95b a 6-member set; adding QA-RA₂-WA gives HT 86a a 6-member set. No other administrative cohort in the corpus shows this level of stability. This strongly suggests a persistent institutional unit receiving grain allocations.

### 2. DA-DU-MA-TA is a New Header Term

This 4-syllable hapax appears in header position on HT 95a (alongside A-DU on 95b and A-KA-RU on 86a). Its unique occurrence suggests a specialized institutional designation. The DA-DU- prefix does not match established DA-MA-TE (Demeter) morphology, but the DA- initial is shared with DA-ME, one of the GRA commodity anchors.

### 3. QE-RA₂-U Receives Below the Mode

Five recipients get 10 (the mode); QE-RA₂-U gets 7. This is the only non-decade quantity and suggests QE-RA₂-U has a different status (smaller estate, newer member, reduced allocation) within the grain distribution group. On HT 95b, QE-RA₂-U is normalized to 10, suggesting the shortfall on 95a may be specific to that accounting period.

### 4. SA-RU as Possible Administrative Term

SA-RU appears on 6 tablets and is a member of the S-R paradigm (SA-RU, SA-RO, SA-RA₂). SA-RA₂ = *šarāku* "allocation" is PROBABLE. SA-RU may be a related form ("allocator"? "allocation-recipient"?) rather than a personal name, explaining its consistently large grain allocations. However, its position among clearly personal names (DI-DE-RU, QE-RA₂-U) favors the name reading.

### 5. Three Different Headers for the Same Group

HT 95a (DA-DU-MA-TA), HT 95b (A-DU), and HT 86a (A-KA-RU + A-DU) all distribute grain to the same recipients. This provides evidence that:
- Multiple institutional sources provide grain to one recipient group
- The same distribution may be recorded under different administrative categories
- The GRA cohort represents a recurring administrative unit, not a one-time allocation
