## Structural Analysis

### Document Type

**Uniform distribution list from A-DU (inferred GRA)**

HT 95b records A-DU distributing exactly 10 units to each of 6 named recipients. The commodity is not explicitly stated (Line 3 damaged) but is inferred as GRA from dual commodity anchors and the partner tablet.

### Document Structure

```
[H]   A-DU                           Header: contributor/sender (CERTAIN)
      𐄁                              Separator
[R]   SA-RU              10          Recipient 1: 10 units
[?]   𐝫  𐄁  𐝫                        Damaged line (commodity logogram? entry?)
[R]   DA-ME              10          Recipient 2: 10 units
[R]   MI-NU-TE           10          Recipient 3: 10 units
[R]   KU-NI-SU           10          Recipient 4: 10 units
[R]   DI-DE-RU           10          Recipient 5: 10 units
[R]   QE-RA₂-U           10          Recipient 6: 10 units
```

### Notable Structural Features

1. **Perfectly uniform distribution**: All 6 recipients receive exactly 10 units. This is unique among analyzed tablets -- no other distribution is perfectly uniform.

2. **A-DU header confirmed**: A-DU as contributor/sender is at CERTAIN confidence (10 corpus-wide, Semitic *adu*). It also appears as header on HT 86a Section 2 (GRA+B to DA-ME and MI-NU-TE).

3. **SA-RU in first position**: On side a, SA-RU is the largest recipient (20). On side b, SA-RU is listed first (position of prominence) but receives the same amount as everyone else.

4. **Damaged Line 3**: The lacuna (𐝫 𐄁 𐝫) could be:
   - A commodity logogram (GRA or variant) that would parallel side a's header structure
   - An additional entry whose name and quantity are lost
   - A sub-category marker (like RU-RA₂ on HT 11b)

5. **Recipient order differs from side a**: Side a: DA-ME, MI-NU-TE, SA-RU, KU-NI-SU, DI-DE-RU, QE-RA₂-U. Side b: SA-RU, DA-ME, MI-NU-TE, KU-NI-SU, DI-DE-RU, QE-RA₂-U. SA-RU moves from position 3 to position 1. The other 5 maintain the same relative order.

### Key Insight: Two Different Administrative Actions for the Same Group

Side a (DA-DU-MA-TA) distributes grain unequally -- SA-RU gets double the mode, QE-RA₂-U gets below. Side b (A-DU) distributes uniformly -- everyone gets 10.

This suggests:
- **Side a**: Reflects actual need or status-based allocation (SA-RU = larger estate; QE-RA₂-U = smaller)
- **Side b**: Reflects a standard/baseline allocation (equal distribution, possibly a ration or quota)
- **Different sources**: DA-DU-MA-TA and A-DU may represent different administrative accounts, budgets, or periods

## Novel Observations

### 1. Perfect Uniformity is Unique

No other analyzed tablet distributes the same quantity to every recipient. This "flat ration" pattern has implications:
- It may represent a periodic standard issue (monthly grain ration?)
- It simplifies accounting (no need for recipient-specific calculations)
- It contrasts with the unequal side a distribution, confirming that different administrative actions produce different allocation patterns for the same group

### 2. A-DU Distributes Both VIR and GRA

A-DU appears as contributor of VIR (HT 85a, 66 workers) and GRA (HT 86a Section 2, HT 95b). This confirms A-DU is not commodity-specific -- it is a general administrative term for "contributor/provider" applicable across commodity types.

### 3. Two-Sided Administration for One Cohort

The same physical tablet records the same group receiving grain from two different sources (DA-DU-MA-TA on side a, A-DU on side b). This is the clearest evidence yet that Minoan scribes maintained parallel accounts for the same recipient group under different administrative categories on a single document.
