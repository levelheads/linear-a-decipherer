## Discrimination Analysis

### Which Hypothesis Should Be ELIMINATED?

**PROTO-GREEK** should be eliminated as a primary language hypothesis.

**Justification**:
1. **Critical phonological mismatch**: /o/ frequency 3.9% vs expected ~20%
2. **Complete absence of Greek morphology**: 0% of expected case endings (-os, -oi, -ai, -es)
3. **Lexical mismatch**: KU-RO is not the Linear B equivalent (to-so)
4. **Only positive**: 1 word with weak cognate support
5. **Negative evidence score**: -15.0 (most negative of all hypotheses)

**Recommendation**: Retain Proto-Greek ONLY as a loan-layer hypothesis for post-1450 BCE contact, not as the underlying language of Linear A inscriptions.

### Which Hypothesis Is STRONGEST?

**SEMITIC (as administrative loan layer)** is the strongest hypothesis.

**Justification**:
1. **Lexical success**: KU-RO, KI-RO, SA-RA2 all have strong Semitic etymologies
2. **Highest aggregate score**: +47.4 from 38-word analysis
3. **Administrative coherence**: Palace economy terminology matches Akkadian/West Semitic
4. **No critical contradictions**: Only lacks genetic morphology (consistent with loans)
5. **Phonological compatibility**: /a/-dominance and CV structure fit

**Important caveat**: Evidence supports Semitic as a **loan layer**, not as the genetic affiliation of Linear A. The absence of triconsonantal morphology prevents claiming genetic relationship.
