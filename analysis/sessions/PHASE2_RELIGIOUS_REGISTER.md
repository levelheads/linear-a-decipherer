## 5. Multi-Hypothesis Analysis

### Hypothesis 1: Luwian/Anatolian

**Supporting Evidence**:
- **-WA-JA ending**: Matches Luwian -wa- particle + -iya adjectival suffix
- **A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA**: Could parse as Luwian A-TA-I + divine element + -WA-JA
- **-JA endings**: Very common in religious texts (cf. Luwian -iya)
- **Gemination SA-SA**: Luwian uses reduplication for emphasis

**Problematic Evidence**:
- No clear Luwian theonyms match JA-SA-SA-RA-ME
- U-NA-KA-NA-SI has no Luwian parallel

**Assessment**: MODERATE FIT (some structural support, weak lexical support)

### Hypothesis 2: Semitic (West Semitic/Akkadian)

**Supporting Evidence**:
- **SA-SA-RA**: Could relate to Semitic *sr root (prince/rule)
- **Compound divine names**: Common in Semitic (cf. Phoenician theophoric names)
- **-ME ending**: Could be Semitic mimation (archaic nominal ending)

**Problematic Evidence**:
- No clear Semitic triconsonantal patterns
- I-PI-NA-MA, SI-RU-TE have no Semitic cognates
- Religious vocabulary structure differs from Semitic patterns

**Assessment**: WEAK FIT (limited support)

### Hypothesis 3: Pre-Greek Substrate

**Supporting Evidence**:
- **Unique vocabulary**: Religious terms are largely hapax or rare
- **Gemination**: Pre-Greek substrate may have had gemination
- **-nth- type patterns absent**: Consistent with non-IE language
- **JA-SA-SA-RA-ME**: Structure resembles proposed Pre-Greek divine names
- **DA-MA-TE**: May be source of Greek Demeter (if Pre-Greek origin)

**Problematic Evidence**:
- Limited comparison material (Pre-Greek is reconstructed)

**Assessment**: GOOD FIT (no contradictions, some structural support)

### Hypothesis 4: Proto-Greek

**Supporting Evidence**:
- **DA-MA-TE**: Could be Proto-Greek *da-mater "earth mother" > Demeter
- **I-PI-NA-MA**: Could contain Greek *-ma suffix

**Problematic Evidence**:
- Very few Greek cognates in religious vocabulary
- /o/ frequency too low for Greek
- No Greek case endings visible
- JA-SA-SA-RA-ME has no Greek etymology

**Assessment**: VERY WEAK FIT (Phase 1 already identified Proto-Greek as weakest hypothesis)

### Hypothesis Ranking for Religious Register

1. **Pre-Greek Substrate** - Best fit; unique vocabulary matches expectations
2. **Luwian/Anatolian** - Moderate fit; structural parallels but weak lexical
3. **Semitic** - Weak fit; some isolated matches
4. **Proto-Greek** - Very weak; only DA-MA-TE offers support
