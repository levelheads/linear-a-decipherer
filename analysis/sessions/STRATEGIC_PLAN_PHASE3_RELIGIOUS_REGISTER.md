## Executive Summary

Phase 3 analyzed the religious vocabulary from 59 inscriptions at peak sanctuaries and cave sites. The analysis confirms a distinct **ritual register** with specialized vocabulary absent from administrative texts.

**Key Findings**:

| Term | Occurrences | Sites | Function | Confidence |
|------|-------------|-------|----------|------------|
| A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA | 11 | 5 sites | Opening invocation | PROBABLE |
| JA-SA-SA-RA-ME | 7 | 5 sites | Divine name | PROBABLE |
| SI-RU-TE | 7 | 5 sites | Ritual closing | POSSIBLE |
| I-PI-NA-MA | 5 | 4 sites | Ritual formula (with SI-RU-TE) | PROBABLE |
| U-NA-KA-NA-SI | 4 | 3 sites | Divine epithet | PROBABLE |

**Canonical Libation Formula** (IOZa2 exemplar):
```
A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA → JA-DI-KI-TU → JA-SA-SA-RA-ME → U-NA-KA-NA-SI → I-PI-NA-MA → SI-RU-TE
```

### 4.2 Morphological Analysis

```
JA - SA - SA - RA - ME
│    └──┬──┘   │    │
│    gemination│    └─ divine suffix (cf. Her-ME-s)
│              └────── unknown root
└─ prefix (article? vocative?)
```

**Key Features**:
1. **Gemination (SA-SA)**: Pre-Greek substrate feature
2. **-ME suffix**: Common in divine names (Hermes, possibly borrowed into Greek)
3. **JA-/A- alternation**: Prefix variation (article vs. direct address?)

## 7. DA-MA-TE (Demeter) Analysis

### 7.1 Attestations

| Site | Form | Context |
|------|------|---------|
| KYZa2 | DA-MA-TE | Kythera standalone |
| ARZf1 | I-DA-MA-TE | Arkalokhori compound |
| ARZf2 | I-DA-MA-TE | Arkalokhori compound |

### 7.2 Linear B Cognate Confirmation

**Linear B**: da-ma-te (Pylos tablets: PY En 609, PY Eo 276)
**Greek**: Δημήτηρ (Demeter)

The Linear A DA-MA-TE is the **earliest attestation of the goddess name**, predating Linear B by ~200-300 years.

**Implication**: Demeter worship has **Minoan origins**, not Greek import.

### 7.3 I-DA-MA-TE Compound

The Arkalokhori form I-DA-MA-TE suggests:
- I- prefix (theophoric element? "to Demeter"?)
- Compound name (person "dedicated to Demeter"?)
