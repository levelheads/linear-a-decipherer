## Executive Summary

This analysis extends morphological understanding beyond the well-established K-R paradigm (KU-RO/KI-RO) by systematically examining the S-R, T-T, D-R paradigms, the -JA suffix distribution, and the -TE/-TI verbal system.

**Key Findings**:

1. **S-R Paradigm**: 38 occurrences across 9 members - HIGH confidence; structural parallel to K-R but functional divergence from SA-RA2
2. **T-T Paradigm**: 11 occurrences across 4 members - HIGH confidence; TA-TI/TE-TU show vowel alternation
3. **D-R Paradigm**: 10 occurrences across 4 members - HIGH confidence; DA-RE dominant at 7 occurrences
4. **-JA Suffix**: 65+ occurrences - PROBABLE adjectival/genitive marker (Luwian -iya parallel)
5. **-TE/-TI Endings**: 56/49 occurrences respectively - POSSIBLE verbal tense markers (Luwian parallel)

**Synthesis**: Linear A employs a systematic vowel alternation system where:
- First syllable vowel (U/I/A) encodes functional category
- Second syllable vowel (O/E/A/U/I) encodes grammatical refinement
- This parallels the K-R paradigm structure but with paradigm-specific semantics

## 6. Synthesis: Vowel Alternation Semantic System

### 6.1 Cross-Paradigm Comparison

| Paradigm | First Vowel Function | Second Vowel Function | Semantic Clarity |
|----------|---------------------|----------------------|------------------|
| **K-R** | U=positive, I=negative | O=main, E=subtotal, A=? | **HIGH** |
| **S-R** | A=dominant, I=rare | RA2=allocation, RU/RO=? | **PARTIAL** |
| **D-R** | A=dominant, U=rare | RE=entry, RA=? | **PARTIAL** |
| **T-T** | A/E alternation | TU/TI/TA/TE | **LOW** |

### 6.2 Vowel Alternation Rules

**First Syllable Vowel**:
| Vowel | K-R Function | S-R Function | D-R Function |
|-------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| U | Positive (totals) | Rare (1 form) | Rare (2 forms) |
| I | Negative (deficits) | Minor (4 forms) | Absent |
| A | Headers/categories | Dominant (33 forms) | Dominant (8 forms) |

**Pattern**: U/I semantic polarity appears **K-R specific**, not universal

**Second Syllable Vowel**:
| Vowel | Proposed Function | Paradigms |
|-------|-------------------|-----------|
| -O | Main/complete form | K-R, S-R |
| -E | Subtotal/intermediate | K-R (KU-RE), D-R (DA-RE) |
| -A | Variant/category | All paradigms |
| -U | Action/stative? | K-R (KA-RU), S-R (SA-RU) |
| -I | Present/active? | T-T (TA-TI) |

### 6.3 Semantic System Assessment

**HYPOTHESIS**: Linear A employs a two-tier vowel alternation system where:

1. **First syllable vowel** encodes category (positive/negative in K-R; neutral in others)
2. **Second syllable vowel** encodes grammatical refinement (case? aspect? tense?)

**EVIDENCE FOR**:
- K-R paradigm shows clear U/I semantic polarity
- All paradigms show -O/-E/-A/-U second syllable variation
- Luwian parallels support vowel-encoded grammar

**EVIDENCE AGAINST**:
- S-R paradigm does NOT show U/I polarity
- D-R paradigm does NOT show U/I polarity
- Functional differentiation in non-K-R paradigms is unclear

**VERDICT**: Vowel alternation is **paradigm-specific**, not a universal system. K-R developed specialized semantic encoding that other paradigms do not fully share.
