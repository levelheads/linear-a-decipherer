## 2. Variation Analysis: Lexical, Dialectal, or Functional?

### The Three Hypotheses

**H1: LEXICAL VARIATION**
- Same functions, different words
- Prediction: Equivalent terms at each site for each function
- Result: **PARTIALLY SUPPORTED** — Some functions show no equivalent (SA-RA₂)

**H2: DIALECTAL VARIATION**
- Same roots with phonological differences
- Prediction: K-R variants (KU-RA, KI-RA) are dialects of KU-RO, KI-RO
- Result: **PARTIALLY SUPPORTED** — Eastern sites show -A endings vs western -O

**H3: FUNCTIONAL VARIATION**
- Different administrative practices requiring different vocabulary
- Prediction: Sites with different economic focus use different terms
- Result: **STRONGLY SUPPORTED** — KH's CYP focus correlates with no K-R forms

### Synthesis

| Variation Type | Evidence | Assessment |
|----------------|----------|------------|
| Lexical | No KH equivalent for KU-RO | LOW |
| **Dialectal** | KU-RA (ZA), KI-RA (ZA) vs KU-RO (HT) | **MEDIUM** |
| **Functional** | HT accounting hub vs KH trade records | **HIGH** |

**Conclusion**: The variation is primarily **FUNCTIONAL** (different transaction types) with secondary **DIALECTAL** overlay (eastern -A vs western -O vowels).

## 3. HT as Central Accounting Hub

### Evidence

1. **Vocabulary Concentration**
   - KU-RO: 35/37 occurrences (94.6%) at HT
   - KI-RO: 16/16 occurrences (100%) at HT
   - SA-RA₂: 20/20 occurrences (100%) at HT

2. **Administrative Sophistication**
   - Three-tier totaling: PO-TO-KU-RO > KU-RO > KU-RE
   - Deficit tracking: KI-RO co-occurring with KU-RO
   - Allocation marking: SA-RA₂ for distributions

3. **Tablet Types**
   - HT: Complex multi-entry ledgers
   - KH: Simple commodity + quantity pairs
   - ZA/PH: Mixed, often single entries

4. **Scribe System**
   - HT: 24+ identified scribes
   - KH: 6 identified scribes
   - ZA: 5 identified scribes

## 4. Chronological Evolution

### KU-RO Distribution by Period

| Period | Total KU-RO | Sites |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| MMII | 0 | — |
| **MMIII** | **1** | **PH** (earliest) |
| LMIA | 0 | — |
| **LMIB** | **36** | HT (35), ZA (1) |

### Interpretation

1. **MMIII (c.1700-1600 BCE)**: KU-RO emerges at Phaistos
2. **LMIA (c.1600-1500 BCE)**: Gap in attestation (preservation?)
3. **LMIB (c.1500-1450 BCE)**: Standardized at HT, spreads to ZA

**Conclusion**: The K-R accounting system may have originated at PH (the earliest site) and was later centralized and elaborated at HT during LMIB.
