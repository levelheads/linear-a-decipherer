## Executive Summary

Building on Phase B1 distributional findings (288 occurrences, 88.2% word-initial), this phase generates specific phoneme candidates for sign *301 and tests them against the libation formula A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA and cross-corpus evidence.

**Key Finding**: The **hybrid sign hypothesis** remains strongest. For the phonetic component, the **pharyngeal fricative /ħa/** (voiceless) emerges as the leading candidate based on:
1. Word-initial positional constraint
2. Absence in Greek phonology (explains Linear B dropping)
3. Akkadian administrative vocabulary parallels
4. Phonotactic fit with Minoan vowel patterns

## Hybrid Sign Model (Recommended)

Based on all evidence, *301 is best understood as a **hybrid sign**:

### Primary Function: Logogram (Administrative)
- Represents unknown commodity or category
- Used standalone with numerals (82.6% of occurrences)
- Parallels VIN, OLE, GRA logograms
- Meaning: Unknown commodity (possibly related to phonetic value)

### Secondary Function: Syllabogram (Religious)
- Represents CV syllable /ħa/ or /kya/
- Used in A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA libation formula
- Appears in ligatures (I+*301, MI+*301)
- Functions phonetically in multi-sign words

### Model Confidence: HIGH

This dual-function model explains:
1. Why 82.6% occurrences are standalone (logographic)
2. Why 17.4% occur in syllabic contexts (phonetic)
3. Why Greeks dropped the sign (both functions became obsolete)
4. Why it has restricted phonotactics (commodity-associated phoneme)
