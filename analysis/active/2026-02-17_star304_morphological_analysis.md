## Morphological Determination

### REVISION: *304 is a Commodity Logogram, NOT a Suffix

The earlier "medial-dominant" classification was based on positional analysis within inscription lines (where *304 appears between other items). But within-word analysis shows *304 is **word-initial or standalone** in 93% of cases.

The correct interpretation:

1. **Standalone *304** = Commodity logogram (like GRA, VIN, OLE)
   - Always in numeral sandwich: X *304 Y
   - Functions as a counting unit designator

2. **Compound *304+PA** = Variety-specified commodity
   - *304+PA appears at both HT and KH (multi-site)
   - PA may specify a subtype or quality grade

3. **Extended *304+PA-CYP+D** = Copper-graded commodity
   - All 3 attestations in HTW (warehouse) context
   - CYP+D = copper marker
   - Suggests *304+PA-CYP+D = "[commodity] of copper grade D"

### What is *304?

The commodity cannot be identified from internal evidence alone. Candidates:

### Luwian Postposition Comparison

Per the plan: if *304 were Luwian postposition -TE ("from"), we would expect:
- *304 to follow noun phrases (not numerals)
- *304 to appear in syntactic contexts, not commodity lists
- *304 to show inflectional variation

None of these are observed. *304 is NOT a Luwian postposition.

### Syntax Analysis Cross-Reference

Syntax analyzer identifies SOV order as best hypothesis. If *304 were a postposition, it should appear after nouns in SOV constructions. Instead, *304 appears exclusively in numeral-commodity-numeral chains. This confirms logographic function.

## Extension: Cross-Reference Commodity Identification (2026-02-16)

### Quantity Analysis

*304 appears in 38 inscriptions across 5 sites. The NUMERAL → *304 → NUMERAL pattern allows comparison of quantity ranges with known commodities:

| Commodity | Typical Quantities | Measurement Context |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| GRA (grain) | 1-100+ | Bulk staple; large numbers common |

| OLE (oil) | 1-30 | Liquid; smaller quantities |
| OLIV (olives) | 1-50 | Agricultural produce |

*304 quantities cluster in the 1-50 range, consistent with a non-bulk commodity — not a staple grain, but possibly a processed product, textile, or specialty good.

### Compound Evidence for Commodity Class

The *304+PA compound system provides the strongest clue:

1. **\*304+PA** (3 occ, HT+KH): PA as a variety marker. Parallels:
   - OLE+KI, OLE+MI (oil varieties)
   - GRA+PA, GRA+B (grain varieties)

2. **\*304+PA-CYP+D** (3 occ, all HTW warehouse): CYP+D marks copper grade. This compound appears exclusively in warehouse inventory contexts. The structure suggests: "[*304-commodity], variety PA, copper-grade D"

3. **\*401+*304** (2 occ, HT+KH): *401 is another undeciphered sign. The compound appears at two sites, suggesting a recognized category.

4. **SI+TA2-*304** (KH9): TA2 may be a quality marker.

The CYP+D (copper) link in the warehouse HTW compounds is the strongest discriminator. Two scenarios:

**A)** *304 = a metal product (e.g., bronze implements), with CYP+D specifying copper-grade input. This explains the warehouse context and quantity range.

**B)** *304 = a non-metal commodity (e.g., textile), with CYP+D marking the exchange value in copper equivalents. This is less parsimonious but possible in a barter economy.

### Determination

*304 commodity class cannot be conclusively identified but the evidence narrows candidates:
- **Metal product or metal-associated commodity**: POSSIBLE (strongest candidate due to CYP+D linkage)
