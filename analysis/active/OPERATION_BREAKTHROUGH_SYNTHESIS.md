## Breakthrough Discoveries

### 1. Closed Syllable Structure in Minoan (V1)

**Discovery**: Sign *118 shows 69% FINAL position — proves Linear A represented closed syllables (CVC).

**Implication**:
- Minoan phonology had word-final consonants
- Greeks dropped these signs because Linear B uses strict CV
- Opens possibility of reconstructing consonant inventory from dropped signs

**Confidence**: HIGH

### 2. Two Distinct Linguistic Registers (V1, V2, V3)

**Discovery**: Administrative and religious vocabulary show different linguistic origins.

| Register | Dominant Hypothesis | Key Terms |
|----------|---------------------|-----------|
| Administrative | Semitic (+47.4) | KU-RO, KI-RO, SA-RA₂ |
| Religious | Luwian/Pre-Greek (+14.5/+13.0) | A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA, JA-SA-SA-RA-ME |

**Implication**: Linear A is NOT monolingual. Different domains preserve different language layers.

**Confidence**: HIGH

### 3. K-R Innovation Horizon = MMIII (V5)

**Discovery**: KU-RO first appears at MMIII Phaistos (PH(?)31a), ~200 years before full K-R system.

| Period | K-R Vocabulary |
|--------|---------------|
| MMII | ZERO |
| MMIII | KU-RO only |
| LMIB | Full system (KU-RO + KI-RO + SA-RA₂) |

**Implication**: Semitic administrative vocabulary adopted gradually. Phaistos may be origin site.

**Confidence**: HIGH

### 4. Khania = Parallel Administrative System (V3)

**Discovery**: Khania has ZERO K-R vocabulary despite being LMIB (contemporary with HT).

| Feature | HT | KH |
|---------|-----|-----|
| K-R terms | Full system | ZERO |
| Focus | Oil/grain/wine | Copper |
| Overlap | — | 1.8% |

**Implication**: K-R is HT-specific, not universal Minoan. KH may preserve indigenous vocabulary.

**Confidence**: HIGH

### 5. Gender Classifier System (V1, V5)

**Discovery**: *21F/*21M are grammatical gender classifiers, not phonemes.

**Evidence**:
- F = feminine marker (animals, personnel)
- M = masculine marker
- Prominent in MMIII (earliest attestations)

**Implication**: Minoan had grammatical gender marked by classifiers (agglutinative/classifier language feature).

**Confidence**: HIGH

### 6. Libation Formula = 6-Position Structure (V2)

**Discovery**: Religious formula has fixed grammatical positions.
