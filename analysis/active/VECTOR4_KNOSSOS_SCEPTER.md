## Executive Summary

The Knossos Scepter (KN Zf 2) has 119 signs — **1.6% of the entire Linear A corpus in ONE object**. It features unique properties: 2 scribes, mixed scripts (Linear A + Hieroglyphic), dual register (ceremonial ring + administrative handle), and a vessel logogram system with PHONETIC LIGATURES.

**Critical Precedent**: MA-RU (A 559) proves that **ligatured signs spell Minoan words phonetically** (MA+RU = *maru* = "wool"). This validates decoding the scepter's vase+ligature system — those ligatures may spell commodity names just like MA-RU spells "wool."

## Vase + Ligature System: The Key to Commodity Decoding

### The MA-RU Precedent

**Established Finding** (Salgarella 2020):
- A 559 = MA+RU (ligatured signs)
- Phonetically spells: *maru*
- Meaning: "wool"
- Linear B retained as *145/LANA (wool logogram)
- Greek borrowed as μαλλός /mallós/

**Implication**: Ligatured CV signs spell Minoan words. The scepter's amphora ligatures may spell commodity names.

### Acrophonic Principle

Linear B uses acrophonic labeling for oil types:
- OLE+WO = *wórdos* "rose" → rose oil
- OLE+RI = *rínon* "linen" → linseed oil
- OLE+PA = *pálaima* "palm" → palm oil

**Test**: Do scepter ligatures follow same pattern?

| Ligature | Linear B Parallel | Possible Minoan Content |
