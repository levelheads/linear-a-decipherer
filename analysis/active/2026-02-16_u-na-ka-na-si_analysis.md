## -SI Suffix Detachability Test

**Critical evidence: TLZa1**

TLZa1 contains the libation formula with U-NA-KA-NA (no -SI) in the same formulaic position where other inscriptions have U-NA-KA-NA-SI. The formula at TLZa1:

> A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA ... JA-SA-SA-RA-ME **U-NA-KA-NA** I-PI-NA-MA SI-RU-TE

Compare IOZa2:

> A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA ... JA-SA-SA-RA-ME **U-NA-KA-NA-SI** I-PI-NA-MA SI-RU-TE

The substitution U-NA-KA-NA ↔ U-NA-KA-NA-SI in an otherwise identical formula proves -SI is a **detachable suffix**, not an integral part of the root.

**Result: CONFIRMED — -SI detaches.**

## Internal Structure

### Morphological Segmentation

```
U - NA - KA - NA - SI
│    │    │    │    └── Suffix (detachable)
│    │    └────┘
│    │    Root: KA-NA
│    └── Linking element or prefix extension
└── Prefix (cf. U-MI-NA-SI, U-*34-SI)
```

Evidence for this segmentation:

1. **U- prefix**: Shared with U-MI-NA-SI (2 occ) and U-*34-SI (3 occ). Three distinct words share both U- prefix and -SI suffix, suggesting a morphological template: U-[ROOT]-SI.

2. **-KA-NA root or element**: Appears in the base form. The -RU- infix in U-NA-RU-KA-NA-SI suggests KA-NA is a coherent unit (the infix precedes it, not interrupts it).

3. **-NA- linking element**: Present between U- and KA-NA. Could be part of a longer prefix U-NA- or a standalone morpheme.

4. **-SI suffix**: Detachable (proven by TLZa1). Functions as a case ending, verbal marker, or adjectival suffix.

### The -RU- Infix

U-NA-RU-KA-NA-SI (IOZa16) shows -RU- inserted between NA and KA-NA:

```
U - NA - KA - NA - SI     (base suffixed form)
U - NA - RU - KA - NA - SI (infixed form)
```

Infixation is rare cross-linguistically. Languages that use productive infixes include:
- Austronesian languages (common)
- Some Pre-Indo-European substrates (proposed)
- NOT typical of Semitic, Luwian, or Proto-Greek

This is potentially diagnostic evidence for Pre-Greek substrate affiliation, though a single contrast pair (U-NA-KA-NA-SI vs U-NA-RU-KA-NA-SI) is insufficient for certainty.

### Compound Form: JA-SA-U-NA-KA-NA-SI (PKZa8)

PKZa8 attests JA-SA-U-NA-KA-NA-SI, which appears to prefix JA-SA to the full word. This parallels the JA-SA-SA-RA-ME pattern — JA-SA may be a formulaic prefix applied to different roots.

## SYZa2: U-NA-KA-NA-SI-OLE

At Syme (SYZa2), the form U-NA-KA-NA-SI-OLE appears — with OLE (oil determinative) appended. This suggests the libation word could take a commodity determinative, potentially specifying the substance being offered. This is the only attestation of a logogram attached to a formula word and may represent a site-specific scribal practice.

## Determination

1. **-SI is a detachable suffix**: CONFIRMED (TLZa1 base form evidence)
2. **U-NA-KA-NA appears independently**: CONFIRMED (TLZa1)
3. **Internal structure shows U-[NA]-KA-NA-SI template**: PROBABLE
4. **-RU- infixation is real**: PROBABLE (one contrast pair; structurally consistent)
5. **Best linguistic affiliation**: Inconclusive — Luwian leads in vocabulary scoring, but Pre-Greek structural features (infixation) are stronger diagnostic markers

**Confidence**: PROBABLE (for morphological analysis); POSSIBLE (for language affiliation)

**Falsification criteria**:
- -SI detachability falsified if new inscriptions show U-NA-KA-NA and U-NA-KA-NA-SI in *different* formulaic positions (not interchangeable)
- -RU- infixation falsified if U-NA-RU-KA-NA-SI proves to be a different word entirely (not a morphological variant)
- Pre-Greek structural argument falsified if other Luwian or Semitic infixation parallels are identified in Linear A
