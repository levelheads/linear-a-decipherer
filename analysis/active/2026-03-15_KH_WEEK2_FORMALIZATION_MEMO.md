## Findings

### 1. KH15 extends NI into mixed KH commodity accounting

`KH15` reads:

```text
CYP 1/2
NI 1/2
NI 1/4
```

This matters for two reasons:

1. **NI co-occurs directly with CYP** inside a short KH administrative tablet.
2. **NI remains fractional at KH**, consistent with KH's transaction-level style.

This does not change the approved HIGH operational status of NI, but it strengthens the argument that NI is not an imported oddity at Khania. It is embedded in the KH ledger layer itself.

### 2. KH25 shows scale without totals

`KH25` preserves:

```text
VIR+[?] 140
VIR+[?] 10
```

Even with damage, the structure is clear enough to support a formalization point:

- KH can record **very large personnel quantities**
- KH still does so **without KU-RO / KI-RO**
- this is consistent with **transaction-level recording of a single account block**, not HT-style balance sheets

### 3. KH54 is the mixed-commodity edge case the model needed

`KH54` preserves:

```text
CYP+E 1
GRA 1
```

This is small but important. It confirms that:

1. KH is not reducible to a pure copper corpus
2. copper-grade accounting can coexist with a non-copper commodity on the same tablet
3. mixed-commodity KH texts still do not require K-R vocabulary

This supports the structural model from `kh_vs_ht_accounting_philosophy.md`: KH differs from HT in **aggregation behavior**, not in the mere ability to mix commodities.

### 4. `*86-RO` remains part of the KH administrative stamp family

`KHWa1013-1016` are all single-form `*86-RO` documents. Combined with the earlier `*86+*188` analysis:

- `*86+*188` is the established KH roundel/receipt stamp family
- `*86-RO` is KH-only and formulaic
- integrated results classify `*86-RO` as **ELIMINATED** for language-level hypothesis testing

That means `*86-RO` is currently a **script-function blocker**, not a language decipherment candidate. The right treatment is structural:

- keep it in Lane C
- do not spend ritual/admin translation capacity trying to lexicalize it
- use it to map the KH administrative stamp subsystem

### 5. CYP grading remains a quality distinction

Nothing in week-2 evidence weakens the current revision:

- `CYP+D`, `CYP+E`, `CYP+K`, and plain `CYP` are best treated as **quality/grade qualifiers**
- the earlier "D = fractions, E = integers" rule remains falsified by prior KH readings
- week-2 packets fit the revised model, not the old one

## Formalization Rules for Week 2-3 KH Work

### Rule A: Do not expect KU-RO / KI-RO on KH admin tablets

Absence of K-R terms at KH is structural, not a missing decipherment gap.

### Rule B: Treat CYP qualifiers as grade markers first

When a KH tablet shows `CYP+X`, default interpretation should be **copper grade/quality**, not a syntax or quantity-format rule.

### Rule C: Treat NI as an active KH commodity line

KH evidence now spans:

- fractional NI (`KH8`, `KH15`)
- small integer NI (`KH11`)
- bulk NI (`KH88`)

This is a scale continuum, not three unrelated uses.

### Rule D: Keep `*86-RO` in blocker-control mode

Do not promote or broadly translate `*86-RO`. Current best use is as a control family for KH stamp administration.

### Rule E: Mixed-commodity KH tablets are legitimate, not anomalies

`KH54` shows that KH can mix copper and grain on one tablet without becoming HT-like.
