## Problem Statement

Pre-Greek hypothesis currently has vocabulary matches but no structural/morphological markers. If -ME and -SI are productive suffixes in the libation formula vocabulary, they would constitute morphological anchors elevating Pre-Greek from vocabulary-only to structural.

## Evidence for -ME as a Suffix

### JA-SA-SA-RA-ME (7 occ, 5 sites)

| Attestation | Site | Text |
|-------------|------|------|
| IOZa2 | Iouktas | JA-DI-KI-TU **JA-SA-SA-RA-ME** U-NA-KA-NA-SI |
| IOZa6 | Iouktas | I-NA-TA-I-ZU-DI-SI-KA **JA-SA-SA-RA-ME** |
| IOZa9 | Iouktas | **JA-SA-SA-RA-ME** |
| PLZf1 | Platanos | U-QE-TI **JA-SA-SA-RA-ME** TA-NU-NI-KI-NA |
| PSZa2 | Psykhro | -JA-TI **JA-SA-SA-RA-ME** |
| TLZa1 | Troullos | **JA-SA-SA-RA-ME** U-NA-KA-NA I-PI-NA-MA |
| PKZa27 | Palaikastro | **JA-SA-SA-RA-ME** U-NA-KA-NA-SI |

### Critical Evidence: IOZa16

**IOZa16**: AROM JA-SA-SA-RA [line break] ME U-NA-RU-KA-NA-SI

The scribe wrote JA-SA-SA-RA and ME on **separate lines**. This is direct physical evidence that -ME is a detachable morpheme, not an integral part of the word. The scribe ran out of space and broke the word at the morpheme boundary.

### Base Form Attestation

| Form | Occ | Interpretation |
|------|-----|---------------|
| JA-SA-SA-RA-ME | 7 | Full form with suffix |
| JA-SA-SA-RA | 1 (IOZa16) | Base form (suffix on next line) |
| SA-SA-RA-ME | 2 | Shortened variant with suffix |
| A-SA-SA-RA-ME | 1 | Variant prefix |
| SA-RA-ME | 1 (IOZa12) | Further abbreviation |

The root appears to be **SA-SA-RA** with:
- JA- prefix (most common, 8/12 forms)
- -ME suffix (detachable)

### -ME Productivity

14 distinct words end in -ME across the corpus (24 total occurrences):
- JA-SA-SA-RA-ME (7), DA-ME (4), SA-SA-RA-ME (2), and 11 hapax forms
- The variety of base forms demonstrates productivity

### Pre-Greek -ME Parallel

Pre-Greek substrate words frequently show -mn- and -m- suffixes (Beekes):
- Labyrinth-os → *labrys + -inth- + -os (Pre-Greek compound)
- Place names in -mn-: Amnissos, Kadmos
- The -ME suffix could represent a Pre-Greek nominal marker

## Evidence for -SI as a Suffix

### U-NA-KA-NA-SI (4 occ, 3 sites)

| Attestation | Site | Context |
|-------------|------|---------|
| IOZa2 | Iouktas | JA-SA-SA-RA-ME **U-NA-KA-NA-SI** I-PI-NA-MA |
| IOZa9 | Iouktas | **U-NA-KA-NA-SI** |
| KOZa1 | Kophinas | **U-NA-KA-NA-SI** |
| PKZa27 | Palaikastro | JA-SA-SA-RA-ME **U-NA-KA-NA-SI** |

### Base Form and Variants

| Form | Occ | Interpretation |
|------|-----|---------------|
| U-NA-KA-NA-SI | 4 | Full form |
| U-NA-KA-NA | 1 (TLZa1) | Base form without -SI |
| U-NA-RU-KA-NA-SI | 1 (IOZa16) | Extended form with -RU- infix |

Key evidence:
1. **TLZa1**: U-NA-KA-NA appears WITHOUT -SI, in the same formula position — proving -SI is detachable
2. **IOZa16**: U-NA-RU-KA-NA-SI shows -RU- infixed between NA and KA-NA — proving internal morphological complexity

### -SI Productivity

28 distinct words end in -SI across the corpus (35 total occurrences):
- U-NA-KA-NA-SI (4), U-MI-NA-SI (2), U-*34-SI (2), and 25 other forms
- High variety demonstrates productivity

### Pre-Greek -SI Parallel

Pre-Greek substrate words show -ss- and -s- suffixes:
- Knoss-os, Amniss-os, Tyliss-os (Pre-Greek -ss- toponym pattern)
- The -SI suffix could represent a Pre-Greek verbal/adjectival marker

## Morphological System Discovery

The libation formula contains a productive morphological system:

| Root | Prefix | Infix | Suffix | Full Form |
|------|--------|-------|--------|-----------|
| SA-SA-RA | JA- | — | -ME | JA-SA-SA-RA-ME |
| SA-SA-RA | A- | — | -ME | A-SA-SA-RA-ME |
| NA-KA-NA | U- | — | -SI | U-NA-KA-NA-SI |
| NA-KA-NA | U- | -RU- | -SI | U-NA-RU-KA-NA-SI |

This shows:
1. **Detachable suffixes** (-ME, -SI) — proven by line-break and unsuffixed base forms
2. **Variable prefixes** (JA-, A-, U-) — likely function markers
3. **Infixation** (-RU- in U-NA-RU-KA-NA-SI) — rare cross-linguistically, diagnostic
4. **Reduplication** (SA-SA in SA-SA-RA) — common in Pre-Greek substrate words

### Reduplication Attestation Breakdown

JA-SA-SA-RA-ME contains SA-SA (apparent reduplication of SA). This claim requires quantification:

| Form | Type | Occ | Independent attestation? |
|------|------|-----|--------------------------|
| JA-SA-SA-RA-ME | Reduplicated + suffix | 7 | Yes — 7 inscriptions, 5 sites |
| SA-SA-RA-ME | Reduplicated + suffix (no JA-) | 2 | Yes — variant without prefix |
| A-SA-SA-RA-ME | Reduplicated + suffix (A- prefix) | 1 | Hapax |
| SA-RA-ME | Non-reduplicated + suffix | 1 (IOZa12) | Hapax — possible abbreviation |
| JA-SA-SA-RA | Reduplicated base (IOZa16 line-break) | 1 | Base form before -ME on next line |

**Hapax caveat**: A-SA-SA-RA-ME (1 occ) and SA-RA-ME (1 occ) are each single attestations. The non-reduplicated form SA-RA-ME is attested only once (IOZa12), so the claim that SA-SA-RA reduces to SA-RA rests on a single attestation and must be treated as tentative. The core reduplication pattern (SA-SA-RA) is robustly attested (10 total occurrences across the JA-SA-SA-RA-ME and SA-SA-RA-ME forms).
