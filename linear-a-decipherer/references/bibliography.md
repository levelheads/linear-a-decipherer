## Primary Corpus & Reference Works

### GORILA (Recueil des inscriptions en linéaire A)

**Godart, Louis, and Jean-Pierre Olivier.** 1976-1985. *Recueil des inscriptions en linéaire A* (GORILA). Études Crétoises 21, vols. 1-5. Paris: Librairie Orientaliste Paul Geuthner.

- **Volume 1** (1976): Tablettes éditées avant 1970
- **Volume 2** (1979): Nodules, scellés et rondelles
- **Volume 3** (1982): Tablettes éditées depuis 1970
- **Volume 4** (1982): Autres documents
- **Volume 5** (1985): Addenda, corrigenda, concordances, index et planches des signes

**Note**: Establishes the AB numbering system (AB 01-AB 131) and graphical templates for Linear A signs. Authoritative corpus through 1985.

---

**Note**: Updated edition with standardized signary formalization.

## Recent Discoveries

### Knossos Ivory Scepter (2024)

**Kanta, Athanasia, Dimitri Nakassis, Thomas G. Palaima, and Massimo Perna.** 2025. "An archaeological and epigraphical overview of some inscriptions found in the Cult Center of the city of Knossos (Anetaki plot)." *Ariadne*, Supplement 5: 27-43.

**Article URL**: https://ejournals.lib.uoc.gr/Ariadne/article/view/1841
**PDF URL**: https://ejournals.lib.uoc.gr/Ariadne/article/view/1841/1751
**DOI**: 10.26248/ariadne.vi.1841
**License**: CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 (Open Access)

**Key findings**:
- **Longest Linear A inscription ever discovered**: 119 signs (84 preserved + 35 partial)
- Two inscriptions by different scribes:
  - **KN Zg 57**: Ivory ring (ceremonial/religious, calligraphic style)
  - **KN Zg 58**: Ivory handle (administrative, with numerals and fractions)
- Context: "Fetish Shrine," Anetaki plot, Knossos (Neopalatial, 1700-1450 BCE)
- Full transliteration forthcoming in "Anetaki II" publication

**Local copy**: `references/pdfs/Kanta_et_al_2025_Knossos_Scepter.pdf`

## Methodology & Decipherment History

### Linear B Decipherment

**Ventris, Michael, and John Chadwick.** 1973. *Documents in Mycenaean Greek* (second edition). Cambridge: Cambridge University Press.

**First edition**: 1956
**Note**: Foundational work on Linear B decipherment demonstrating methodology applicable to Linear A.

---

**First edition**: 1958
**ISBN**: 978-0-521-39830-4

**Note**: Historical account of the Linear B decipherment process, emphasizing the role of pattern analysis (Kober's work) and the importance of methodological rigor over preconceived theories.

**DOI**: 10.2307/499077
**JSTOR**: https://www.jstor.org/stable/499077

---

**Kober, Alice E.** 1945. "Inflection in Linear Class B: 2. Declension (continued)." *American Journal of Archaeology* 49 (2): 143-151.
**DOI**: 10.2307/499692
**JSTOR**: https://www.jstor.org/stable/499692

---

**Kober, Alice E.** 1946. "The Minoan Scripts: Fact and Theory." *American Journal of Archaeology* 52 (1): 82-103.
**DOI**: 10.2307/500265
**JSTOR**: https://www.jstor.org/stable/500265

**Key quote** (p. 93):
> "Anyone who took a theory about the language as their starting point was doomed to failure. The single route to the finishing line was to find the patterns and deep symmetries inside the primary texts."

**Note**: Establishes the **Kober Principle** (First Principle #1): pattern analysis must precede language assumptions. Her Grid Method demonstrated inflectional patterns without knowing the language, enabling Ventris's later success.

## Linguistic Hypotheses

### Hypothesis 1: Luwian / Anatolian

**Palmer, Leonard R.** 1958. "Luwian and Linear A." *Transactions of the Philological Society* 57 (1): 75-100.
**DOI**: 10.1111/j.1467-968X.1958.tb00289.x

**Note**: Pioneering proposal that Linear A records an Anatolian language related to Luwian, based on morphological parallels and geographic proximity. Cites Bronze Age Anatolia-Crete trade connections.

---

**Finkelberg, Margalit.** 1998. "The Language of Linear A: Greek, Semitic, or Anatolian?" In *Recherches récentes en épigraphie crétoise*, ed. by Jan Driessen and Alexandre Farnoux, 311-318. Bulletin de Correspondance Hellénique, Supplément 33. Athens: École française d'Athènes.

**ISBN**: 978-2-86958-121-9

**Note**: Argues for Anatolian affiliation based on phonological and morphological evidence; proposes specific Luwian cognates for Linear A sequences.

---

**Younger, John G., and Paul Rehak.** 2008. "Technical Observations on the Inscriptions." In *Corpus der minoischen und mykenischen Siegel, Band V, Teil 3: Iraklion Archäologisches Museum, Die Siegelabdrücke von Knossos*, ed. by John Younger and Paul Rehak, 312-324. Mainz: Philipp von Zabern.

**Note**: Technical analysis supporting Anatolian hypothesis through sign usage patterns.

---

### Hypothesis 2: Semitic

**Gordon, Cyrus H.** 1966. *Evidence for the Minoan Language.* Ventnor, NJ: Ventnor Publishers.

**Reprinted**: 1982 (revised edition)

**Note**: Proposes that Linear A records a Semitic language related to Akkadian or West Semitic. Cites lexical parallels, including proposed reading of ku-ro as Semitic *kull* "all, total." Controversial but systematically argued.

---

**Best, Jan G. P.** 1972. "The Phaistos Disc—An Account of Progress on the Problem of its Texts." In *Kadmos* 11 (1): 66-83.
**DOI**: 10.1515/kadm.1972.11.1.66

---

**Best, Jan G. P., and Fred Woudhuizen.** 1988. *Ancient Scripts from Crete and Cyprus.* Leiden: E. J. Brill.
**ISBN**: 978-90-04-08431-2

**Note**: Comparative analysis of Aegean scripts; includes Semitic hypothesis argumentation with focus on phonological correspondences.

---

### Hypothesis 3: Pre-Greek Substrate

**Beekes, Robert S. P.** 2014. *Pre-Greek: Phonology, Morphology, Lexicon.* Edited by Stefan Norbruis. Leiden / Boston: Brill.
**ISBN**: 978-90-04-27883-5
**DOI**: 10.1163/9789004278844

**Note**: Comprehensive study of Pre-Greek substrate language(s) in ancient Greece. Proposes that Linear A may record a language ancestral to or related to the substrate visible in later Greek loanwords. Analyzes phonological patterns incompatible with Greek or Indo-European.

---

**Furnée, Edzard J.** 1972. *Die wichtigsten konsonantischen Erscheinungen des Vorgriechischen: Mit einem Appendix über den Vokalismus.* The Hague: Mouton.
**ISBN**: 978-90-279-2232-8

**Note**: Foundational work on Pre-Greek phonology; establishes patterns (consonant alternations, unusual clusters) that may be reflected in Linear A if it records the substrate language.

---

### Hypothesis 4: Proto-Greek / Early Greek

**Georgiev, Vladimir.** 1963. *Les deux langues des inscriptions crétoises en linéaire A.* Sofia: Académie Bulgare des Sciences.

**Note**: Proposes that Linear A records an early form of Greek distinct from Mycenaean Greek. Argues for dual-language hypothesis: administrative texts in Proto-Greek, religious texts in Pre-Greek substrate.

---

**Mosenkis, Iurii.** 2019. "Linear A *64 ku-ni-su and Related Forms." *Studia Linguistica Universitatis Iagellonicae Cracoviensis* 136: 13-25.
**DOI**: 10.4467/20834624SL.19.002.10227

**Note**: Recent argument for Greek interpretation of specific Linear A sequences based on morphological parallels with Mycenaean Greek.

## Citation Conventions

When citing inscriptions, use the following format:

**Tablets/Documents**: Site code + Number (e.g., HT 13, KH 7, ZA 4, PH 1)
**2024 discoveries**: KN Zg 57 (scepter ring), KN Zg 58 (scepter handle)

**Site codes**:
- **HT** = Hagia Triada
- **KH** = Khania
- **ZA** = Zakros
- **PH** = Phaistos
- **KN** = Knossos
- **MA** = Malia
- **AR** = Arkalochori
- **PE** = Petras
- **TY** = Tylissos

**Suffix conventions**:
- **W** = sealing/nodule (*W*ax)
- **Z** = stone vessel (*Z*as in German for stone)

**Example citation**:
"The sequence ku-ro appears in administrative contexts (HT 13, HT 95, KH 7) with consistent positional distribution at list endings, suggesting a totaling function analogous to Linear B *ku-ro* (Godart & Olivier 1976: vol. 1, 38-40)."

## Usage Notes

1. **Prefer primary sources** over secondary interpretations when citing evidence
2. **GORILA** is the authoritative reference for AB sign numbers and inscription transcriptions
3. **SigLA** is the most current online resource for paleographic variants
4. **lineara.xyz** provides the most comprehensive structured data for computational analysis
5. Always cite **page numbers** when quoting or referencing specific claims
